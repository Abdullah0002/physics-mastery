NEET Physics has 45 questions worth 180 marks. Scoring 160+ requires knowing exactly where NTA focuses its attention — and where it does not.

Here is what five years of question analysis (2019–2024) reveals.

## What Changed Between 2022 and 2024

**Modern Physics increased significantly.** Photoelectric effect, Bohr model, and nuclear physics questions jumped from 2–3 to 4–5 per paper in 2023 and 2024. Students still allocating minimal time to modern physics are leaving 16–20 marks on the table.

**Oscillations decreased.** Once a reliable source of 3–4 questions per paper, oscillations and waves now routinely contribute just 2. Still important, but no longer a priority for time-constrained students.

**Electrostatics is now mandatory.** NTA has tested multi-concept electrostatics problems (field + potential + capacitor) in every paper from 2021 to 2024. Skipping this chapter is no longer an option even for NEET aspirants.

## The NEET-Specific Pattern

NEET Physics tests **application**, not derivation. You will not be asked to derive Faraday's law — you will be asked what happens to the induced EMF when coil area doubles and field halves.

The typical question structure is:
1. State a physical situation
2. Ask for a numerical answer or the correct relationship

This means **formula fluency** and **unit analysis** matter more in NEET than in JEE. You should be able to look at a question and identify which formula applies within 10 seconds.

## Priority Chapters for NEET 2025

Based on current trend analysis, allocate study time in roughly this ratio:

**High priority (60% of Physics preparation time):**
Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Laws of Motion, Modern Physics, Moving Charges & Magnetism

**Medium priority (30%):**
Kinematics, Work-Energy-Power, Ray Optics, Thermodynamics

**Lower priority (10%):**
Gravitation, Oscillations, Waves, Semiconductors (but never skip entirely)

## One More Observation

Questions that appeared in JEE Main 2022 or 2023 often appear in NEET 2024 or 2025 with minor modifications. Solving JEE Main PYQs alongside NEET PYQs gives you a 15–20% larger practice pool without extra effort.
