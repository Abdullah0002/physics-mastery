## 1. Capacitance

A capacitor stores charge. Capacitance $C$ relates charge to voltage:

<Formula label="Capacitance">
$$C = \frac{Q}{V} \quad \text{(SI unit: farad, F = C/V)}$$
</Formula>

---

## 2. Parallel Plate Capacitor

Two plates of area $A$ separated by distance $d$ (with vacuum):

<Formula label="Capacitance — Parallel Plate">
$$C_0 = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$$
</Formula>

With dielectric (dielectric constant $K$):

<Formula label="With Dielectric">
$$C = KC_0 = \frac{K\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$$
</Formula>

**Electric field inside (uniform):** $E = V/d = \sigma/\varepsilon_0 = \sigma K/\varepsilon$ (with dielectric: $E = V/d = Q/(KC_0 d)$).

---

## 3. Spherical Capacitor

Isolated sphere of radius $R$:

$$C = 4\pi\varepsilon_0 R$$

Concentric spheres ($a < b$):

$$C = 4\pi\varepsilon_0\frac{ab}{b-a}$$

---

## 4. Cylindrical Capacitor

Two coaxial cylinders, inner radius $a$, outer radius $b$, length $L$:

$$C = \frac{2\pi\varepsilon_0 L}{\ln(b/a)}$$

---

## 5. Combinations of Capacitors

### Series

$$\frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \cdots$$

Same charge $Q$ on each. Voltages add: $V = V_1 + V_2$.

### Parallel

$$C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2 + \cdots$$

Same voltage $V$ across each. Charges add: $Q = Q_1 + Q_2$.

<Callout type="tip">
For two capacitors in series: $C_{eq} = C_1C_2/(C_1+C_2)$ and the voltage divides inversely with capacitance ($V_1/V_2 = C_2/C_1$).
</Callout>

---

## 6. Energy Stored in a Capacitor

<Formula label="Energy Stored">
$$U = \frac{1}{2}QV = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{Q^2}{2C}$$
</Formula>

**Energy density in the electric field:**

$$u = \frac{U}{Ad} = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 = \frac{1}{2}K\varepsilon_0 E^2 \text{ (with dielectric)}$$

---

## 7. Dielectrics in Capacitors

When a dielectric is inserted (isolated capacitor, $Q$ constant):
- $C$ increases by factor $K$
- $V$ decreases by factor $K$
- $E$ decreases by factor $K$
- $U$ decreases by factor $K$

When dielectric is inserted with battery connected ($V$ constant):
- $C$ increases by factor $K$
- $Q$ increases by factor $K$
- $E$ unchanged
- $U$ increases by factor $K$

<Callout type="important">
These two scenarios give opposite energy results! With isolated capacitor: inserting dielectric releases energy (it's pulled in). With battery: energy increases (battery must supply extra charge).
</Callout>

---

## 8. Charge Sharing Between Capacitors

When two charged capacitors ($C_1, Q_1$) and ($C_2, Q_2$) are connected (positive to positive):

$$V_{common} = \frac{Q_1+Q_2}{C_1+C_2}$$

Energy lost (as heat in resistance):

$$\Delta U = \frac{C_1 C_2(V_1-V_2)^2}{2(C_1+C_2)}$$

---

## 9. RC Circuit

When a capacitor $C$ charges through resistance $R$ from voltage $V_0$:

<Formula label="Charging">
$$q(t) = Q_0\left(1 - e^{-t/RC}\right), \quad V_C = V_0\left(1-e^{-t/RC}\right)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Discharging">
$$q(t) = Q_0\,e^{-t/RC}, \quad V_C = V_0\,e^{-t/RC}$$
</Formula>

**Time constant:** $\tau = RC$. After time $\tau$: capacitor is ~63% charged.

---

## 10. Summary

| Quantity | Formula |
|---|---|
| Parallel plate $C$ | $\varepsilon_0 A/d$ |
| With dielectric | $K\varepsilon_0 A/d$ |
| Series combo | $1/C_{eq} = \Sigma(1/C_i)$ |
| Parallel combo | $C_{eq} = \Sigma C_i$ |
| Energy stored | $\frac{1}{2}CV^2 = Q^2/2C$ |
| Energy density | $\frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$ |
| RC time constant | $\tau = RC$ |
