## Types of Friction

<Formula label="Static Friction (up to maximum)">
$$f_s \leq \mu_s N$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Maximum Static Friction (limiting friction)">
$$f_{s,\max} = \mu_s N$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Kinetic (Sliding) Friction">
$$f_k = \mu_k N$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Relationship between coefficients">
$$\mu_s > \mu_k$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
Kinetic friction is independent of speed and area of contact (for standard JEE/NEET problems).
</Callout>

---

## Angle of Friction and Angle of Repose

<Formula label="Angle of Friction λ">
$$\tan\lambda = \mu_s \implies \lambda = \arctan(\mu_s)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Resultant of Normal and Friction">
$$F_{\text{resultant}} = \sqrt{N^2 + f^2} = \frac{N}{\cos\lambda}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Angle of Repose α">
$$\tan\alpha = \mu_s \implies \alpha = \lambda$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="tip">
The angle of repose equals the angle of friction. A block just starts to slip when the incline angle equals the angle of repose.
</Callout>

---

## Inclined Plane

<Formula label="Block at Rest on Incline — Friction condition">
$$f = mg\sin\theta,\quad N = mg\cos\theta \quad (\theta \leq \alpha)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Acceleration Down Incline (kinetic friction)">
$$a = g(\sin\theta - \mu_k\cos\theta)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Acceleration Up Incline (friction opposes motion)">
$$a = -g(\sin\theta + \mu_k\cos\theta)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Minimum force to move block up incline">
$$F_{\min} = mg\sin\theta + \mu_s mg\cos\theta = mg(\sin\theta + \mu_s\cos\theta)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Minimum force to push block at angle φ to horizontal">
$$F_{\min} = \frac{\mu_s mg}{\sqrt{1+\mu_s^2}} = mg\sin\lambda \quad \text{(applied at angle }\lambda\text{ above horizontal)}$$
</Formula>

## Pulling vs Pushing

<Formula label="Normal Force while Pulling at angle θ">
$$N = mg - F\sin\theta$$
$$\implies f = \mu(mg - F\sin\theta)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Normal Force while Pushing at angle θ">
$$N = mg + F\sin\theta$$
$$\implies f = \mu(mg + F\sin\theta)$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="tip">
Pulling is easier than pushing because pulling reduces the normal force and hence friction.
</Callout>

---

## Two-Block Problems (Friction between blocks)

<Formula label="Acceleration (no sliding between blocks — F applied to lower block m1)">
$$a = \frac{F}{m_1 + m_2}, \quad f_{12} = \frac{m_2 F}{m_1 + m_2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Condition for no sliding">
$$f_{12} \leq \mu_s m_2 g \implies F \leq \mu_s(m_1 + m_2)g$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="When blocks slide relative to each other">
$$a_1 = \frac{F - \mu_k m_2 g}{m_1},\quad a_2 = \mu_k g$$
</Formula>

---

## Rolling Friction

<Formula label="Rolling Friction Force">
$$f_r = \mu_r N = \frac{\mu_r mg}{R} \cdot R = \mu_r mg$$
$$\text{(where } \mu_r \text{ is coefficient of rolling friction, much smaller than } \mu_k\text{)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Hierarchy of friction coefficients">
$$\mu_r \ll \mu_k < \mu_s$$
</Formula>

---

## Circular Motion with Friction

<Formula label="Maximum speed on a circular road (no banking)">
$$v_{\max} = \sqrt{\mu_s R g}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Minimum and Maximum speed on banked road with friction">
$$v_{\min} = \sqrt{Rg\tan(\theta - \lambda)}, \quad v_{\max} = \sqrt{Rg\tan(\theta + \lambda)}$$
$$\text{where } \tan\lambda = \mu_s$$
</Formula>
