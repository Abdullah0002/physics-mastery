## 1. Blackbody Radiation and Planck's Hypothesis

A **blackbody** absorbs all incident radiation. Planck proposed energy is quantised:

$$E = nh\nu, \quad h = 6.626\times10^{-34}\ \text{J·s}$$

This resolved the ultraviolet catastrophe. Wien's law and Stefan's law follow from Planck's distribution.

---

## 2. Photoelectric Effect (Einstein, 1905)

Light hits a metal → electrons are ejected (photoelectrons).

**Observations:**
- Photoelectric effect occurs only if $\nu \geq \nu_0$ (threshold frequency) — independent of intensity.
- Number of photoelectrons $\propto$ intensity.
- Max KE of photoelectrons is independent of intensity but depends on frequency.

<Formula label="Einstein's Photoelectric Equation">
$$KE_{max} = h\nu - \phi = h(\nu - \nu_0)$$
</Formula>

$\phi = h\nu_0$ = work function of the metal.

<Formula label="Stopping Potential">
$$eV_s = KE_{max} = h\nu - \phi$$
</Formula>

**Plank's constant from experiment:**

$$h = \frac{e(V_{s2}-V_{s1})}{\nu_2-\nu_1}$$

## 3. Photon Properties

| Property | Formula |
|---|---|
| Energy | $E = h\nu = hc/\lambda$ |
| Momentum | $p = h\nu/c = h/\lambda$ |
| Mass | 0 (rest mass); $E/c^2$ effective |

## 4. Compton Effect

X-rays scattered by electrons have increased wavelength:

<Formula label="Compton Shift">
$$\Delta\lambda = \lambda' - \lambda = \frac{h}{m_e c}(1-\cos\phi)$$
</Formula>

$h/m_e c = 2.426\ \text{pm}$ (Compton wavelength). Confirms photon has momentum.

---

## 5. de Broglie Wavelength

Every particle has an associated wavelength:

<Formula label="de Broglie Wavelength">
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
</Formula>

For a particle accelerated through potential $V$:

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2meV}}$$

<Callout type="tip">
For electron at 100 V: $\lambda \approx 1.23/\sqrt{V}$ Å $= 0.123$ nm. This is atomic-scale — justifies electron diffraction.
</Callout>

---

## 6. Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

$$\Delta x\cdot\Delta p_x \geq \frac{h}{4\pi}$$

$$\Delta E\cdot\Delta t \geq \frac{h}{4\pi}$$

You cannot simultaneously know position and momentum with arbitrary precision.

## 7. Bohr's Model of Hydrogen Atom

<Step n={1}>Electrons orbit the nucleus in allowed circular orbits without radiating.</Step>
<Step n={2}>Angular momentum is quantised: $mvr_n = nh/(2\pi)$.</Step>
<Step n={3}>Radiation emitted/absorbed when electron transitions between orbits: $E_{photon} = E_i - E_f$.</Step>

<Formula label="Radius of nth orbit">
$$r_n = n^2 a_0 = n^2\times0.529\ \text{Å} \quad \text{(hydrogen)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Energy of nth orbit">
$$E_n = -\frac{13.6}{n^2}\ \text{eV} \quad \text{(hydrogen)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Speed in nth orbit">
$$v_n = \frac{v_1}{n} = \frac{2.18\times10^6}{n}\ \text{m/s}$$
</Formula>

## 8. Spectral Series of Hydrogen

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H Z^2\left(\frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2}\right), \quad R_H = 1.097\times10^7\ \text{m}^{-1}$$

| Series | $n_1$ | Region |
|---|---|---|
| Lyman | 1 | UV |
| Balmer | 2 | Visible |
| Paschen | 3 | IR |
| Brackett | 4 | Far IR |
| Pfund | 5 | Far IR |

---

## 9. X-rays

**Continuous X-rays (Bremsstrahlung):** deceleration of electrons. Min wavelength:

$$\lambda_{min} = \frac{hc}{eV}$$

**Characteristic X-rays:** transitions between inner electron shells. Gives sharp lines ($K_\alpha$, $K_\beta$, etc.).

**Moseley's law:** $\sqrt{\nu} = a(Z - b)$ (links characteristic X-ray frequency to atomic number $Z$).
