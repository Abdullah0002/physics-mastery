## 1. Magnetic Flux

<Formula label="Magnetic Flux">
$$\Phi_B = \int\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{A} = BA\cos\theta$$
</Formula>

SI unit: weber (Wb = T·m²).

---

## 2. Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction

<Formula label="Faraday's Law">
$$\mathcal{E} = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$$
</Formula>

For $N$ turns:

$$\mathcal{E} = -N\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$$

The **negative sign** reflects Lenz's law.

---

## 3. Lenz's Law

The induced EMF always opposes the **change** in flux that caused it. The induced current creates a magnetic field opposing the change.

<Callout type="important">
Lenz's law is a statement of energy conservation. The opposing force from induced currents means you must do work to change the flux — this work goes into electrical energy.
</Callout>

---

## 4. Motional EMF

A conductor of length $L$ moving with velocity $v$ perpendicular to magnetic field $B$:

<Formula label="Motional EMF">
$$\mathcal{E} = BLv$$
</Formula>

More generally: $\mathcal{E} = \int(\vec{v}\times\vec{B})\cdot d\vec{l}$

---

## 5. Eddy Currents

Induced currents in bulk conductors (not isolated wires). They:
- Oppose motion (cause **magnetic braking**)
- Generate heat ($I^2R$)
- Are minimised by laminating cores (transformer cores, motors)

---

## 6. Self-Inductance

When current $I$ changes in a coil, it induces EMF in itself:

<Formula label="Self-Inductance">
$$\mathcal{E} = -L\frac{dI}{dt}$$
</Formula>

where $L$ = self-inductance (henry, H). Also: $\Phi = LI$.

**Inductance of solenoid** ($n$ turns/m, length $l$, area $A$):

$$L = \mu_0 n^2 lA = \mu_0 n^2 V$$

---

## 7. Mutual Inductance

EMF induced in coil 2 due to changing current in coil 1:

$$\mathcal{E}_2 = -M\frac{dI_1}{dt}$$

where $M$ = mutual inductance (H). $M = \Phi_{21}/I_1$.

**Coefficient of coupling:** $M = k\sqrt{L_1 L_2}$, $0 \leq k \leq 1$.

---

## 8. Energy Stored in Inductor

<Formula label="Energy in Inductor">
$$U = \frac{1}{2}LI^2$$
</Formula>

**Energy density in magnetic field:**

$$u = \frac{B^2}{2\mu_0}$$

---

## 9. LC Circuit (Free Oscillations)

An LC circuit oscillates with angular frequency:

<Formula label="LC Oscillation frequency">
$$\omega_0 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{LC}}, \quad T = 2\pi\sqrt{LC}$$
</Formula>

Energy oscillates between electric (capacitor) and magnetic (inductor) — analogous to SHM.

---

## 10. LR Circuit

When switch closes, current builds up:

$$I(t) = \frac{\mathcal{E}}{R}\left(1 - e^{-t/\tau_L}\right), \quad \tau_L = \frac{L}{R}$$

When switch opens (current decays):

$$I(t) = I_0\,e^{-t/\tau_L}$$

---

## 11. Transformer

**Ideal transformer** (no losses):

<Formula label="Transformer ratio">
$$\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}, \quad \frac{I_s}{I_p} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
</Formula>

**Power conservation (ideal):** $V_p I_p = V_s I_s$

- Step-up: $N_s > N_p$ → $V_s > V_p$
- Step-down: $N_s < N_p$ → $V_s < V_p$

**Efficiency:** $\eta = P_s/P_p = V_sI_s/(V_pI_p)$
