## Newton's Laws

<Formula label="Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia)">
$$\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = 0 \implies \vec{v} = \text{constant}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Newton's Second Law">
$$\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = m\vec{a} = \frac{d\vec{p}}{dt}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Newton's Third Law">
$$\vec{F}_{AB} = -\vec{F}_{BA}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Linear Momentum">
$$\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Impulse">
$$\vec{J} = \vec{F}\,\Delta t = \Delta\vec{p} = m(\vec{v}_f - \vec{v}_i)$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
Newton's laws are valid only in inertial (non-accelerating) reference frames.
</Callout>

## Free Body Diagram Essentials

<Formula label="Weight">
$$W = mg \quad (g \approx 9.8\ \text{m s}^{-2} \approx 10\ \text{m s}^{-2}\ \text{for JEE})$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Normal Force on Horizontal Surface">
$$N = mg \quad (\text{no vertical acceleration})$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Normal Force on Incline (no acceleration)">
$$N = mg\cos\theta$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Acceleration Along Incline (smooth)">
$$a = g\sin\theta$$
</Formula>

---

## Connected Bodies — Strings & Pulleys

<Formula label="Two Blocks on Horizontal Surface (force F applied to m1)">
$$a = \frac{F}{m_1 + m_2}, \quad T = \frac{m_2 F}{m_1 + m_2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Atwood Machine">
$$a = \frac{(m_1 - m_2)g}{m_1 + m_2}, \quad T = \frac{2m_1 m_2 g}{m_1 + m_2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Block on Incline connected over Pulley">
$$a = \frac{m_1 g - m_2 g\sin\theta}{m_1 + m_2}, \quad T = \frac{m_1 m_2 g(1 + \sin\theta)}{m_1 + m_2}$$
</Formula>

---

## Wedge-Block System

<Formula label="Block on Smooth Wedge (wedge fixed)">
$$a_{\text{block along incline}} = g\sin\theta$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Block and Wedge both Free (smooth surfaces)">
$$a_{\text{wedge}} = \frac{mg\sin\theta\cos\theta}{M + m\sin^2\theta}$$
$$a_{\text{block w.r.t. wedge}} = \frac{(M+m)g\sin\theta}{M + m\sin^2\theta}$$
</Formula>

---

## Pseudo Force (Non-Inertial Frame)

<Formula label="Pseudo Force">
$$\vec{F}_{\text{pseudo}} = -m\vec{a}_{\text{frame}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Apparent Weight in Accelerating Lift">
$$N = m(g \pm a) \quad (+\ \text{for upward acceleration},\ -\ \text{for downward})$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Weightlessness Condition">
$$a = g\ \text{downward} \implies N = 0$$
</Formula>

---

## Spring Force

<Formula label="Hooke's Law">
$$F = -kx$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Spring Combination — Series">
$$\frac{1}{k_{\text{eff}}} = \frac{1}{k_1} + \frac{1}{k_2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Spring Combination — Parallel">
$$k_{\text{eff}} = k_1 + k_2$$
</Formula>

---

## Equations of Motion (Constant Acceleration)

<Formula label="First Equation">
$$v = u + at$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Second Equation">
$$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Third Equation">
$$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Displacement in nth Second">
$$s_n = u + a\left(n - \frac{1}{2}\right)$$
</Formula>

---

## Constraint Relations

<Formula label="String Constraint (inextensible string)">
$$\sum (\text{component of velocity along string}) = 0$$
$$\text{e.g., } v_1 + v_2 = 0\ (\text{for simple pulley})$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Wedge Constraint">
$$v_{\text{block,x}} = v_{\text{wedge}} \quad (\text{horizontal component equal when surface contact maintained})$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="tip">
Draw a fresh FBD for each body separately. Label all forces including normal reactions at every contact surface.
</Callout>
