Ninety days is enough to go from 40 marks to 80+ marks in JEE Main Physics — if the plan is built correctly. Most students plan by chapter. The better way is to plan by **phase**, since each phase builds on the last.

## The Three Phases

| Phase | Duration | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Foundation | Days 1–30 | Concept clarity, all Class 11 chapters |
| Application | Days 31–65 | Class 12, PYQs, chapter-level mocks |
| Exam Readiness | Days 66–90 | Full mocks, revision, weak-spot drilling |

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1–30)

**Daily time:** 3–4 hours on Physics

**Week 1–2 — Mechanics I:**
- Kinematics (3 days): derive equations of motion, projectile, relative velocity
- Laws of Motion (4 days): FBD, friction, constraint motion, pseudo-force

**Week 3 — Mechanics II:**
- Work, Energy & Power (3 days)
- Centre of Mass & Collision (2 days)
- Circular Motion (2 days)

**Week 4 — Mechanics III + Waves:**
- Rotational Dynamics (3 days)
- Gravitation (2 days)
- Oscillations & Waves (2 days)

**Phase 1 milestone:** Solve 30 mixed Mechanics PYQs (2019–2024) without peeking. Target: 60%+ accuracy.

---

## Phase 2: Application (Days 31–65)

**Daily time:** 4–5 hours on Physics

**Week 5–6 — Electrodynamics I:**
- Electrostatics (5 days): this is the most important chapter, do not rush
- Capacitors (2 days)

**Week 7 — Electrodynamics II:**
- Current Electricity (4 days): Kirchhoff's laws, Wheatstone bridge
- Moving Charges & Magnetism (3 days)

**Week 8 — Electrodynamics III:**
- Electromagnetic Induction (3 days)
- AC Circuits (2 days)
- Electromagnetic Waves (2 days)

**Week 9 — Optics + Modern:**
- Ray Optics (3 days)
- Wave Optics (2 days)
- Modern Physics (2 days)

**Phase 2 milestone:** Attempt two chapter-wise mock tests per day. Review every error the same evening.

---

## Phase 3: Exam Readiness (Days 66–90)

**Daily time:** Full mock + analysis (4–6 hours)

**Week 10–11 — Mock testing:**
- 3 full-length JEE Main mocks per week (timed, NTA pattern)
- Immediately identify: which chapter? which concept type? how much time spent?
- Maintain a 3-column error log: chapter / mistake type / correct approach

**Week 12 — Targeted revision:**
- Identify your bottom 3 chapters from mock analysis
- Dedicate 2 full days to each
- Re-solve all PYQs from those chapters

**Day 88–90 — Final consolidation:**
- Review your formula sheets (never the full textbook)
- Re-read your error log — this is your personalised exam guide
- One light mock on Day 89; no new topics on Day 90

## Daily Schedule Template

| Time Block | Activity |
|---|---|
| 07:00–09:00 | New concept study + derivations |
| 09:30–11:30 | Problem solving (new chapter) |
| 14:00–15:30 | PYQ practice (previous chapter) |
| 16:00–17:00 | Error log review + formula revision |

---

## The Rules That Make This Work

1. **Never leave a chapter until you can solve a PYQ you have not seen before without hints.** Familiarity is not mastery.
2. **One missed day is fine. Two in a row is a crisis.** Do not let gaps compound.
3. **Your error log is your most important document.** More valuable than any notes or textbook.
