## Angular Quantities

<Formula label="Angular Displacement">
$$\theta = \frac{s}{r} \quad \text{(radians)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Angular Velocity">
$$\omega = \frac{d\theta}{dt} = \frac{v}{r}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Angular Acceleration">
$$\alpha = \frac{d\omega}{dt} = \frac{a_t}{r}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Frequency and Period Relations">
$$\omega = 2\pi f = \frac{2\pi}{T}, \quad T = \frac{1}{f}$$
</Formula>

## Equations of Circular Motion (Constant Î±)

<Formula label="Rotational Kinematics">
$$\omega = \omega_0 + \alpha t$$
$$\theta = \omega_0 t + \frac{1}{2}\alpha t^2$$
$$\omega^2 = \omega_0^2 + 2\alpha\theta$$
</Formula>

---

## Centripetal Acceleration and Force

<Formula label="Centripetal Acceleration">
$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = \omega^2 r = v\omega$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Centripetal Force">
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = m\omega^2 r$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Tangential Acceleration">
$$a_t = r\alpha = \frac{dv}{dt}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Net Acceleration">
$$a = \sqrt{a_c^2 + a_t^2}$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
Centripetal force is not a new type of force â€” it is the net inward force provided by existing forces (tension, gravity, normal, friction, etc.).
</Callout>

## Conical Pendulum

<Formula label="Angle of Inclination">
$$\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{rg} = \frac{\omega^2 r}{g}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Time Period of Conical Pendulum">
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L\cos\theta}{g}} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{h}{g}}$$
$$\text{(where } h = L\cos\theta \text{ is vertical height)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Tension in String">
$$T_s = \frac{mg}{\cos\theta}$$
</Formula>

---

## Banking of Roads

<Formula label="Ideal Banking Speed (no friction)">
$$v_{\text{ideal}} = \sqrt{Rg\tan\theta}$$
$$\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{Rg}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Maximum Speed on Banked Road with Friction">
$$v_{\max} = \sqrt{Rg\cdot\frac{\tan\theta + \mu_s}{1 - \mu_s\tan\theta}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Minimum Speed on Banked Road with Friction">
$$v_{\min} = \sqrt{Rg\cdot\frac{\tan\theta - \mu_s}{1 + \mu_s\tan\theta}}$$
</Formula>

## Vertical Circle

<Formula label="Speed at angle Î¸ from bottom">
$$v_\theta^2 = v_{\text{bottom}}^2 - 2gR(1-\cos\theta)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Tension at angle Î¸ from bottom">
$$T = \frac{mv_\theta^2}{R} + mg\cos\theta \quad (\text{at bottom reference})$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Tension at Bottom">
$$T_{\text{bottom}} = \frac{mv_b^2}{R} + mg$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Tension at Top">
$$T_{\text{top}} = \frac{mv_t^2}{R} - mg$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Minimum Speed at Top for Complete Circle">
$$v_{\text{top,min}} = \sqrt{gR} \quad (T = 0)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Minimum Speed at Bottom for Complete Circle">
$$v_{\text{bottom,min}} = \sqrt{5gR}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Relation between Bottom and Top Speeds">
$$v_b^2 = v_t^2 + 4gR$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Tension Difference (Bottom âˆ’ Top)">
$$T_{\text{bottom}} - T_{\text{top}} = 6mg$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="tip">
For a bead on the inside of a loop: condition for losing contact is $N = 0$, giving same $v_{\min} = \sqrt{gR}$ at top.
</Callout>

## Death Well / Cyclist in Loop

<Formula label="Minimum Speed at Top of Loop (inside)">
$$v_{\min} = \sqrt{gR}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Normal Force at any point (inside loop)">
$$N = \frac{mv^2}{R} - mg\cos\phi \quad (\phi \text{ from top})$$
</Formula>

## Relative Angular Motion

<Formula label="Angular Velocity of Line Joining Two Bodies">
$$\omega_{\text{rel}} = \frac{v_\perp}{r} = \frac{v\sin\theta}{r}$$
</Formula>
