## 1. Elastic Properties

### Stress and Strain

<Formula label="Stress">
$$\sigma = \frac{F}{A} \quad \text{(Pa = N/m}^2\text{)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Strain">
$$\epsilon = \frac{\Delta L}{L} \quad \text{(dimensionless)}$$
</Formula>

**Types of stress/strain:**
- Longitudinal (tensile/compressive)
- Shear (tangential force)
- Volume (bulk compression)

### Young's Modulus

<Formula label="Young's Modulus">
$$Y = \frac{\text{Longitudinal stress}}{\text{Longitudinal strain}} = \frac{F L}{A \Delta L}$$
</Formula>

### Bulk Modulus

$$B = -\frac{P}{\Delta V/V} = \frac{\text{Volume stress}}{\text{Volume strain}}$$

**Compressibility:** $\kappa = 1/B$

### Shear Modulus (Modulus of Rigidity)

$$\eta = \frac{\text{Shear stress}}{\text{Shear strain}} = \frac{F/A}{\theta}$$

**Relation (for isotropic material):** $Y = 3B(1-2\sigma_P)$, $Y = 2\eta(1+\sigma_P)$ where $\sigma_P$ = Poisson's ratio.

## 2. Elastic Potential Energy

Energy stored in a stretched/compressed wire:

<Formula label="Elastic PE in wire">
$$U = \frac{1}{2}\cdot\text{stress}\cdot\text{strain}\cdot\text{volume} = \frac{F^2 L}{2AY}$$
</Formula>

## 3. Fluid Statics

### Pressure

$$P = \frac{F}{A}, \quad P = \rho g h \quad (\text{gauge pressure at depth }h)$$

Absolute pressure $= P_{atm} + \rho g h$.

### Pascal's Law

Pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted equally in all directions. Used in hydraulic press:

$$\frac{F_1}{A_1} = \frac{F_2}{A_2}$$

### Archimedes' Principle

Buoyant force $= $ weight of fluid displaced:

$$F_b = \rho_{fluid}\cdot V_{submerged}\cdot g$$

**Flotation:** Body floats when $F_b = mg$, i.e., $\rho_{body}/\rho_{fluid} = V_{submerged}/V_{total}$.

---

## 4. Surface Tension

Surface tension $T$ = force per unit length along a surface (N/m), or energy per unit area (J/m²).

**Excess pressure inside:**

<Formula label="Excess Pressure — Soap Bubble (two surfaces)">
$$\Delta P = \frac{4T}{R}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Excess Pressure — Liquid Drop or Air Bubble in liquid (one surface)">
$$\Delta P = \frac{2T}{R}$$
</Formula>

### Capillary Rise

$$h = \frac{2T\cos\theta}{\rho g r}$$

where $\theta$ = contact angle, $r$ = radius of tube. Water in glass: $\theta \approx 0°$ (rise). Mercury in glass: $\theta > 90°$ (depression).

---

## 5. Viscosity

Viscous force between layers of fluid:

$$F = \eta A \frac{dv}{dy}$$

where $\eta$ = coefficient of dynamic viscosity (Pa·s), $dv/dy$ = velocity gradient.

### Stokes' Law

Drag force on a sphere of radius $r$ moving at velocity $v$ in a viscous fluid:

$$F_{drag} = 6\pi\eta r v$$

### Terminal Velocity

When drag + buoyancy = weight:

<Formula label="Terminal Velocity (sphere)">
$$v_t = \frac{2r^2(\rho_s - \rho_f)g}{9\eta}$$
</Formula>

---

## 6. Bernoulli's Theorem

For steady, incompressible, non-viscous fluid flow:

<Formula label="Bernoulli's Equation">
$$P + \frac{1}{2}\rho v^2 + \rho g h = \text{constant}$$
</Formula>

### Continuity Equation

$$A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2 \quad (\text{volume flow rate constant})$$

### Applications

| Application | Key result |
|---|---|
| Venturimeter | $v \propto 1/\sqrt{A}$ at constriction |
| Torricelli's theorem | $v = \sqrt{2gh}$ (hole in tank) |
| Lift on aerofoil | $P_{bottom} > P_{top}$ (higher $v$ on top) |

<Callout type="warning">
Bernoulli applies only to **ideal** fluids (non-viscous, incompressible, streamline flow). Real fluids lose energy to viscosity and turbulence.
</Callout>
