## 1. Reflection of Light

**Laws of reflection:**
1. Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection (both measured from normal).
2. Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal are coplanar.

---

## 2. Spherical Mirrors

Sign convention: distances measured from the pole (P). Distances in the direction of incident light are positive.

<Formula label="Mirror Formula">
$$\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{2}{R}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Magnification">
$$m = -\frac{v}{u} = \frac{h_i}{h_o}$$
</Formula>

| Mirror | $f$ | Image (object beyond $C$) |
|---|---|---|
| Concave | Negative (real focus) | Real, inverted |
| Convex | Positive | Always virtual, upright, diminished |

---

## 3. Refraction — Snell's Law

<Formula label="Snell's Law">
$$n_1\sin\theta_1 = n_2\sin\theta_2$$
</Formula>

$$n = \frac{c}{v} = \frac{\lambda_{vacuum}}{\lambda_{medium}}$$

---

## 4. Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

When light goes from denser ($n_1$) to rarer ($n_2$) medium:

<Formula label="Critical Angle">
$$\sin\theta_c = \frac{n_2}{n_1} \quad (n_1 > n_2)$$
</Formula>

For $\theta > \theta_c$: TIR occurs. Applications: optical fibre, prism binoculars, diamonds.

---

## 5. Refraction at Spherical Surface

Single refracting surface separating media $n_1$ and $n_2$ (radius $R$):

<Formula label="Refraction at Spherical Surface">
$$\frac{n_2}{v} - \frac{n_1}{u} = \frac{n_2 - n_1}{R}$$
</Formula>

---

## 6. Thin Lens Formula

<Formula label="Lens Formula">
$$\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Lensmaker's Equation">
$$\frac{1}{f} = (n-1)\left(\frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2}\right)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Magnification">
$$m = \frac{v}{u}$$
</Formula>

| Lens | $f$ | Converging/Diverging |
|---|---|---|
| Convex | Positive | Converging |
| Concave | Negative | Diverging |

**Two thin lenses in contact:** $\dfrac{1}{f} = \dfrac{1}{f_1} + \dfrac{1}{f_2}$, Power $P = P_1 + P_2$ (dioptre, D).

---

## 7. Prism

For a prism (apex angle $A$, refractive index $n$):

**At minimum deviation $D_m$:**

<Formula label="Prism — Minimum Deviation">
$$n = \frac{\sin\left(\frac{A+D_m}{2}\right)}{\sin(A/2)}$$
</Formula>

At $D_m$: ray passes symmetrically through prism, $r_1 = r_2 = A/2$.

**Thin prism:** $D = (n-1)A$ (small angle approximation).

**Angular dispersion:** $D_{blue} - D_{red} = (n_b - n_r)A$

**Dispersive power:** $\omega = (n_b - n_r)/(n_{mean} - 1)$

---

## 8. Optical Instruments

### Simple Microscope (magnifier)

$$M = \frac{D}{f} + 1 \approx \frac{D}{f} \quad (D = 25\ \text{cm})$$

### Compound Microscope

$$M = m_o \times m_e = \frac{L}{f_o}\cdot\frac{D}{f_e}$$

### Telescope (refracting, normal adjustment)

<Formula label="Telescope Magnification">
$$M = -\frac{f_o}{f_e}$$
</Formula>

Length of tube: $f_o + f_e$ (normal adjustment).

---

## 9. Power of a Lens

$$P = \frac{1}{f(\text{metres})} \quad \text{(dioptre, D)}$$

Concave lens: negative power. Convex: positive.

---

## 10. Summary

| Formula | Use |
|---|---|
| $1/v + 1/u = 1/f$ | Mirrors |
| $1/v - 1/u = 1/f$ | Lenses |
| Snell: $n_1\sin\theta_1 = n_2\sin\theta_2$ | Refraction |
| Critical angle: $\sin\theta_c = n_2/n_1$ | TIR |
| Prism: $n = \sin((A+D_m)/2)/\sin(A/2)$ | Minimum deviation |
| Telescope: $M = f_o/f_e$ | Magnification |
