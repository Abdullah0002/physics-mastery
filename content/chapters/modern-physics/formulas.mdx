## Compton Scattering

<Formula label="Compton Shift">
$$\Delta\lambda = \lambda' - \lambda = \frac{h}{m_e c}(1 - \cos\phi) = \lambda_c(1-\cos\phi)$$
$$\lambda_c = \frac{h}{m_e c} = 2.426\times10^{-12}\ \text{m} = 2.426\ \text{pm}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Maximum Compton Shift (Backscattering)">
$$\Delta\lambda_{\max} = 2\lambda_c = 4.85\ \text{pm} \quad (\phi = 180Â°)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Scattered Photon Energy">
$$E' = \frac{E}{1 + \frac{E}{m_e c^2}(1-\cos\phi)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Kinetic Energy of Recoil Electron">
$$T = E - E' = h\nu - h\nu'$$
</Formula>

## Pair Production and Annihilation

<Formula label="Pair Production (Threshold)">
$$h\nu \geq 2m_e c^2 = 2 \times 0.511\ \text{MeV} = 1.022\ \text{MeV}$$
$$\gamma \to e^- + e^+ \quad \text{(requires nucleus nearby to conserve momentum)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Pair Annihilation">
$$e^+ + e^- \to 2\gamma$$
$$\text{Each photon has energy } m_e c^2 = 0.511\ \text{MeV (when at rest)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Energy-Mass Equivalence">
$$E = mc^2, \quad m_e c^2 = 0.511\ \text{MeV}, \quad m_p c^2 = 938.3\ \text{MeV}$$
$$1\ u \cdot c^2 = 931.5\ \text{MeV}$$
</Formula>

## Photoelectric Effect (Detailed)

<Formula label="Einstein's Equation (with work function)">
$$h\nu = \phi_0 + KE_{\max} = \phi_0 + eV_s$$
$$\phi_0 = h\nu_0 = \frac{hc}{\lambda_0}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Saturation Current (Photocurrent)">
$$I_{\text{sat}} \propto \text{Intensity of light} \propto n_{\text{photons}}$$
$$\text{(independent of frequency above threshold)}$$
</Formula>

## Special Theory of Relativity

<Formula label="Lorentz Factor">
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}} \geq 1$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Time Dilation">
$$\Delta t = \gamma\,\Delta t_0 \quad \text{(moving clock runs slower)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Length Contraction">
$$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma} \quad \text{(moving rod is shorter)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Relativistic Mass-Energy">
$$E = \gamma m_0 c^2 = \sqrt{(pc)^2 + (m_0 c^2)^2}$$
$$KE = (\gamma - 1)m_0 c^2$$
$$E^2 = (pc)^2 + (m_0c^2)^2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Relativistic Momentum">
$$p = \gamma m_0 v = \frac{m_0 v}{\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}}$$
</Formula>

## Nuclear Reactions

<Formula label="Q-Value of Reaction">
$$\text{A + B} \to \text{C + D} + Q$$
$$Q = [(m_A + m_B) - (m_C + m_D)]c^2$$
$$Q = \text{KE}_{\text{products}} - \text{KE}_{\text{reactants}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Threshold Energy for Endothermic Reaction">
$$E_{\text{threshold}} = \frac{|Q|(M+m)}{M}$$
$$\text{(M = target mass at rest, m = projectile mass)}$$
</Formula>

## Fission Energy

<Formula label="Energy Released per Fission (U-235)">
$$Q \approx 200\ \text{MeV} = 3.2\times10^{-11}\ \text{J}$$
$$\text{Power of 1 kg U-235 fission} \approx 8.2\times10^{13}\ \text{J}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Critical Mass Concept">
$$\text{Multiplication factor: } k = \frac{\text{neutrons in generation }n+1}{\text{neutrons in generation }n}$$
$$k > 1 \text{: supercritical; } k = 1 \text{: critical; } k < 1 \text{: subcritical}$$
</Formula>
