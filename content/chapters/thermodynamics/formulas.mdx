## First Law of Thermodynamics

<Formula label="First Law">
$$\Delta U = Q - W$$
$$dU = dQ - dW, \quad dW = P\,dV$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Internal Energy (Ideal Gas)">
$$\Delta U = nC_v\Delta T \quad \text{(valid for ANY process of ideal gas)}$$
</Formula>

---

## Thermodynamic Processes

### Isothermal Process ($T = $ const)

<Formula label="Isothermal: ΔU and Q">
$$\Delta U = 0, \quad Q = W$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Isothermal Work Done by Gas">
$$W = nRT\ln\frac{V_2}{V_1} = nRT\ln\frac{P_1}{P_2} = 2.303\,nRT\log\frac{V_2}{V_1}$$
</Formula>

---

### Adiabatic Process ($Q = 0$)

<Formula label="Adiabatic Condition">
$$PV^\gamma = \text{const}, \quad TV^{\gamma-1} = \text{const}, \quad T P^{(1-\gamma)/\gamma} = \text{const}$$
$$P_1 V_1^\gamma = P_2 V_2^\gamma$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Adiabatic Work Done">
$$W = \frac{P_1V_1 - P_2V_2}{\gamma - 1} = \frac{nR(T_1 - T_2)}{\gamma - 1} = -\Delta U$$
</Formula>

---

### Isochoric Process ($V = $ const)

<Formula label="Isochoric: W and Q">
$$W = 0, \quad Q = \Delta U = nC_v\Delta T$$
</Formula>

---

### Isobaric Process ($P = $ const)

<Formula label="Isobaric Work Done">
$$W = P\Delta V = nR\Delta T$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Isobaric Heat">
$$Q = nC_p\Delta T, \quad \Delta U = nC_v\Delta T$$
</Formula>

---

### Polytropic Process ($PV^n = $ const)

<Formula label="Polytropic Work Done">
$$W = \frac{P_1V_1 - P_2V_2}{n-1} = \frac{nR(T_1-T_2)}{n-1}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Effective Specific Heat for Polytropic">
$$C = C_v\frac{\gamma - n}{1-n} = C_v - \frac{R}{n-1}$$
</Formula>

| Process | n |
|---------|---|
| Isothermal | 1 |
| Adiabatic | γ |
| Isochoric | ∞ |
| Isobaric | 0 |

---

## Summary of Processes

| Process | Q | W | ΔU |
|---------|---|---|-----|
| Isothermal | $nRT\ln(V_2/V_1)$ | $nRT\ln(V_2/V_1)$ | 0 |
| Adiabatic | 0 | $nC_v(T_1-T_2)$ | $nC_v(T_2-T_1)$ |
| Isochoric | $nC_v\Delta T$ | 0 | $nC_v\Delta T$ |
| Isobaric | $nC_p\Delta T$ | $nR\Delta T$ | $nC_v\Delta T$ |

## Second Law and Carnot Engine

<Formula label="Carnot Efficiency">
$$\eta_{\text{Carnot}} = 1 - \frac{T_L}{T_H} = \frac{T_H - T_L}{T_H}$$
$$\text{(temperatures in Kelvin)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Carnot COP (Refrigerator)">
$$\text{COP} = \frac{Q_L}{W} = \frac{T_L}{T_H - T_L}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Carnot COP (Heat Pump)">
$$\text{COP}_{\text{HP}} = \frac{Q_H}{W} = \frac{T_H}{T_H - T_L} = \text{COP}_{\text{ref}} + 1$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
No real engine can have efficiency greater than the Carnot engine operating between the same two temperatures. $\eta_{\text{Carnot}}$ is a function of temperatures ONLY.
</Callout>

## Entropy

<Formula label="Entropy Change">
$$\Delta S = \frac{Q_{\text{rev}}}{T} = \int \frac{dQ_{\text{rev}}}{T}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Entropy Change for Ideal Gas">
$$\Delta S = nC_v\ln\frac{T_2}{T_1} + nR\ln\frac{V_2}{V_1}$$
$$\Delta S = nC_p\ln\frac{T_2}{T_1} - nR\ln\frac{P_2}{P_1}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Second Law (Entropy Statement)">
$$\Delta S_{\text{universe}} \geq 0 \quad \text{(= 0 for reversible, > 0 for irreversible)}$$
</Formula>

## P-V Diagrams

<Formula label="Work = Area under P-V Curve">
$$W = \int P\,dV = \text{Area under curve in P-V diagram}$$
$$\text{(positive for expansion, negative for compression)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Efficiency from Indicator Diagram">
$$\eta = \frac{W_{\text{net}}}{Q_{\text{absorbed}}} = \frac{\text{Area enclosed in cycle}}{Q_{\text{absorbed}}}$$
</Formula>
