Physics is the most score-elastic subject in JEE Main. A student who scores 40 in Chemistry can rarely push it to 80 — but in Physics, going from 40 to 80 in three months is not just possible, it is routine. Here is the framework.

## Know the Weightage Before You Open a Book

JEE Main Physics has a predictable distribution across its 30 questions:

| Area | Approx. Questions | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanics (Class 11) | 8–10 | 27–33% |
| Electrodynamics | 7–9 | 23–30% |
| Optics & Modern Physics | 5–6 | 17–20% |
| Thermodynamics & Waves | 4–5 | 13–17% |

This tells you immediately: **master Mechanics and Electrodynamics first**. Together they account for more than half the paper.

## The Chapter Priority Order

Rank chapters by: (weightage × your current accuracy). Attack the highest product first.

**Tier 1 — Must-master:**
Kinematics, Laws of Motion, Work-Energy, Current Electricity, Electrostatics, Moving Charges & Magnetism, Ray Optics.

**Tier 2 — High return:**
Rotational Motion, Gravitation, EMI & AC Circuits, Wave Optics, Modern Physics.

**Tier 3 — Consolidate last:**
Thermodynamics, KTG, Semiconductors, Communication.

## The 3-Phase Study Model

**Phase 1 — Concept building (4 weeks)**
For each chapter: read theory → derive every formula → solve 20 NCERT-level problems. Do **not** touch JEE-level questions yet. Your brain needs the foundation before the stress test.

**Phase 2 — JEE practice (6 weeks)**
Now attempt PYQ question banks chapter by chapter. For each wrong answer, write down:
1. The concept you misapplied
2. The correct approach in one line

This error log is more valuable than any notes you will ever take.

**Phase 3 — Mock tests (4 weeks)**
Three full-length mocks per week. Analyse each test within 24 hours. Track: *Which chapter? Which concept type? How much time did I spend?*

## The Single Most Important Habit

Solve one **numerical** problem from scratch every day — no looking at hints, no partial attempts. Put your pen down and finish it. This builds the mental stamina that drops under exam pressure if untrained.

## What 90+ Scorers Actually Do Differently

They do not know more chapters. They know fewer chapters **perfectly**. A student who has solved every type of electrostatics question that has ever appeared in JEE Main (2002–2025) will score better than someone who has superficially studied the entire syllabus twice.

**Depth over breadth.** Always.
