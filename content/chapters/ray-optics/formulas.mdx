## Reflection — Plane Mirrors

<Formula label="Angle of Reflection">
$$\angle i = \angle r$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Number of Images in Two Mirrors at Angle θ">
$$n = \frac{360°}{\theta} - 1 \quad \text{(if } 360°/\theta \text{ is even)}$$
$$n = \frac{360°}{\theta} \quad \text{(if } 360°/\theta \text{ is odd and object not on bisector)}$$
</Formula>

---

## Reflection — Curved Mirrors

<Formula label="Mirror Formula">
$$\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{2}{R}$$
$$\text{Sign convention: object on left, distances measured from pole}$$
$$\text{Real is negative (for objects and images on same side as incoming light)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Linear Magnification — Mirror">
$$m = -\frac{v}{u} = \frac{f}{f - u} = \frac{f - v}{f}$$
$$|m| > 1 \text{: enlarged; } |m| < 1 \text{: diminished}$$
$$m > 0 \text{: virtual/erect; } m < 0 \text{: real/inverted}$$
</Formula>

---

## Refraction — Snell's Law

<Formula label="Snell's Law">
$$n_1\sin\theta_1 = n_2\sin\theta_2, \quad \frac{\sin\theta_i}{\sin\theta_r} = \frac{n_2}{n_1} = \mu_{12}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Refractive Index">
$$n = \frac{c}{v} = \frac{\lambda_{\text{vacuum}}}{\lambda_{\text{medium}}} = \frac{\sin\theta_i}{\sin\theta_r}$$
$$n_1\lambda_1 = n_2\lambda_2 \quad \text{(frequency unchanged)}$$
</Formula>

---

## Total Internal Reflection

<Formula label="Critical Angle">
$$\sin\theta_c = \frac{n_2}{n_1} = \frac{1}{\mu} \quad \text{(when } n_1 > n_2\text{)}$$
$$\theta_c = \arcsin\!\left(\frac{1}{\mu}\right)$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
TIR occurs when: (1) light travels from denser to rarer medium AND (2) angle of incidence $> \theta_c$.
</Callout>

---

## Refraction at Spherical Surface

<Formula label="Refraction at Spherical Interface">
$$\frac{n_2}{v} - \frac{n_1}{u} = \frac{n_2 - n_1}{R}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Lateral Magnification at Spherical Surface">
$$m = \frac{n_1 v}{n_2 u}$$
</Formula>

---

## Lens Formulas

<Formula label="Thin Lens Formula">
$$\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Lensmaker's Equation">
$$\frac{1}{f} = (n-1)\left(\frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2}\right)$$
$$\text{(Cartesian sign convention)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Lens Power">
$$P = \frac{1}{f(\text{in metres})} \quad \text{[Dioptres, D]}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Lens Magnification">
$$m = \frac{v}{u} = \frac{f}{f+u} = \frac{f-v}{f}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Lenses in Contact">
$$\frac{1}{f_{\text{eq}}} = \frac{1}{f_1} + \frac{1}{f_2}, \quad P_{\text{eq}} = P_1 + P_2$$
</Formula>

## Displacement Method for Focal Length

<Formula label="Focal Length by Displacement Method">
$$f = \frac{D^2 - d^2}{4D}$$
$$\text{(D = distance between object and screen, d = distance between two positions of lens)}$$
$$m_1 m_2 = 1 \quad \text{(magnifications for two positions)}$$
</Formula>

---

## Prism

<Formula label="Angle of Deviation in Prism">
$$\delta = (i_1 + i_2) - A, \quad A = r_1 + r_2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Minimum Deviation">
$$\delta_m = 2i_m - A, \quad r_1 = r_2 = A/2\ \text{(at min. dev.)}$$
$$n = \frac{\sin\!\left(\frac{A+\delta_m}{2}\right)}{\sin(A/2)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Thin Prism (Small A)">
$$\delta = (n-1)A$$
</Formula>

---

## Dispersion

<Formula label="Angular Dispersion">
$$\Delta\delta = \delta_v - \delta_r = (n_v - n_r)A = \overline{\omega}A$$
$$\overline{\omega} = \frac{n_v - n_r}{n_{\text{mean}} - 1} \quad \text{(dispersive power)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Achromatic Combination (two prisms)">
$$A_1\omega_1 = -A_2\omega_2 \implies \frac{A_1}{A_2} = -\frac{\omega_2}{\omega_1}$$
</Formula>

## Optical Instruments

<Formula label="Simple Microscope — Magnification">
$$M = 1 + \frac{D}{f} \quad \text{(image at near point)}$$
$$M = \frac{D}{f} \quad \text{(image at infinity)}$$
$$D = 25\ \text{cm (least distance of distinct vision)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Compound Microscope — Magnification">
$$M = m_o \times m_e = \frac{L}{f_o}\left(1 + \frac{D}{f_e}\right)$$
$$M_{\text{relaxed eye}} = \frac{L}{f_o}\cdot\frac{D}{f_e}$$
$$\text{(L = tube length, } f_o, f_e \text{ = focal lengths of objective and eyepiece)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Astronomical Telescope — Magnification">
$$M = -\frac{f_o}{f_e} \quad \text{(image at infinity)}$$
$$M = -\frac{f_o}{f_e}\left(1 + \frac{f_e}{D}\right) \quad \text{(image at near point)}$$
$$\text{Tube length: } L = f_o + f_e$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Resolving Power of Telescope">
$$\text{Limit of resolution: } \theta = \frac{1.22\lambda}{D}$$
$$\text{Resolving power} = \frac{D}{1.22\lambda}$$
</Formula>
