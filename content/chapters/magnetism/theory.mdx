## 1. Magnetic Force on Moving Charge (Lorentz Force)

<Formula label="Lorentz Force">
$$\vec{F} = q\vec{v}\times\vec{B}, \quad F = qvB\sin\theta$$
</Formula>

- Direction: right-hand rule (or left-hand for electrons).
- Magnetic force does **no work** (perpendicular to velocity).
- Does not change speed, only direction.

---

## 2. Motion of Charged Particle in a Magnetic Field

If $\vec{v}\perp\vec{B}$: circular motion.

<Formula label="Radius of circular path">
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Time period (independent of velocity)">
$$T = \frac{2\pi m}{qB}, \quad f_c = \frac{qB}{2\pi m}$$
</Formula>

$f_c$ = cyclotron frequency.

**Cyclotron:** Charged particle accelerated in a cyclotron. Max KE when radius = $r_{max}$:

$$KE_{max} = \frac{q^2B^2r_{max}^2}{2m}$$

---

## 3. Force on a Current-Carrying Conductor

<Formula label="Force on current element">
$$\vec{F} = I\vec{L}\times\vec{B} = IL B\sin\theta$$
</Formula>

**Force between two parallel current-carrying wires (separation $d$, length $l$):**

$$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 l}{2\pi d}$$

- Currents in same direction: attract.
- Currents in opposite direction: repel.

---

## 4. Biot–Savart Law

<Formula label="Biot-Savart Law">
$$d\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{Id\vec{l}\times\hat{r}}{r^2}$$
</Formula>

$\mu_0 = 4\pi\times10^{-7}\ \text{T·m/A}$.

### Magnetic Field from Common Configurations

| Source | Location | $B$ |
|---|---|---|
| Long straight wire | At distance $r$ | $\mu_0 I/(2\pi r)$ |
| Circular loop, radius $R$ | At centre | $\mu_0 I/(2R)$ |
| Circular loop | On axis at $x$ | $\frac{\mu_0 IR^2}{2(R^2+x^2)^{3/2}}$ |
| Semicircular arc | At centre | $\mu_0 I/(4R)$ |
| Solenoid (infinite) | Inside | $\mu_0 nI$ |

---

## 5. Ampere's Law

<Formula label="Ampere's Law">
$$\oint \vec{B}\cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_{enc}$$
</Formula>

Used when there is enough symmetry (solenoid, toroid, long wire).

**Toroid (N turns, radius R):**
- Inside (within winding): $B = \mu_0 NI/(2\pi r) = \mu_0 nI$
- Outside: $B = 0$

---

## 6. Torque on Current Loop (Magnetic Dipole)

<Formula label="Torque on current loop">
$$\vec{\tau} = \vec{m}\times\vec{B}, \quad \tau = mB\sin\theta$$
</Formula>

**Magnetic dipole moment:** $m = NIA$ (direction: right-hand rule for current direction).

**PE:** $U = -\vec{m}\cdot\vec{B} = -mB\cos\theta$

---

## 7. Galvanometer, Ammeter, Voltmeter

**Galvanometer** → **Ammeter:** Add shunt $S$ in **parallel**.

$$S = \frac{I_g G}{I - I_g}$$

**Galvanometer** → **Voltmeter:** Add resistance $R$ in **series**.

$$R = \frac{V}{I_g} - G$$

---

## 8. Magnetic Properties of Matter

| Property | $\chi_m$ (susceptibility) | Behaviour |
|---|---|---|
| Diamagnetic | $-1 \leq \chi_m < 0$ | Weakly repelled; $B_{inside} < B_{applied}$ |
| Paramagnetic | $0 < \chi_m \ll 1$ | Weakly attracted |
| Ferromagnetic | $\chi_m \gg 1$ | Strongly attracted; domains; hysteresis |

**Curie's Law (paramagnetic):** $\chi_m = C/T$

**Curie temperature:** Above this, ferromagnetic → paramagnetic.

---

## 9. Bar Magnet

**Axial (end-on) field:**
$$B_{axial} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{2Md}{(d^2-l^2)^2} \approx \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{2M}{d^3} \quad (d \gg l)$$

**Equatorial (broad-side) field:**
$$B_{eq} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{M}{(d^2+l^2)^{3/2}} \approx \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{M}{d^3} \quad (d \gg l)$$

where $M = 2ml$ = magnetic dipole moment.
