## 1. Temperature Scales

| Scale | Freezing | Boiling | Absolute zero |
|---|---|---|---|
| Celsius (°C) | 0 | 100 | −273.15 |
| Fahrenheit (°F) | 32 | 212 | −459.67 |
| Kelvin (K) | 273.15 | 373.15 | 0 |

<Formula label="Conversion">
$$K = °C + 273.15, \quad °F = \frac{9}{5}°C + 32$$
</Formula>

## 2. Thermal Expansion

### Linear Expansion

<Formula label="Linear Expansion">
$$\Delta L = L_0\,\alpha\,\Delta T \Rightarrow L = L_0(1 + \alpha\Delta T)$$
</Formula>

### Area Expansion

$$\Delta A = A_0\,\beta\,\Delta T, \quad \beta \approx 2\alpha$$

### Volume Expansion

$$\Delta V = V_0\,\gamma\,\Delta T, \quad \gamma \approx 3\alpha$$

<Callout type="important">
The relation $\gamma = 3\alpha$ (and $\beta = 2\alpha$) holds for **isotropic** materials. For liquids, only volume expansion is defined ($\gamma_{apparent} = \gamma_{liquid} - \gamma_{container}$).
</Callout>

### Anomalous Expansion of Water

Water expands on cooling from 4°C to 0°C. Maximum density at **4°C**. This causes ice to float and lakes to freeze from the top.

## 3. Calorimetry

**Specific Heat Capacity $c$:** Heat needed to raise 1 kg by 1 K.

$$Q = mc\Delta T$$

**Heat of Fusion $L_f$:** Heat needed to melt 1 kg at constant temperature.

$$Q = mL_f$$

**Heat of Vaporisation $L_v$:** Heat needed to vaporise 1 kg.

$$Q = mL_v$$

**Principle of calorimetry (no heat loss):**

$$\text{Heat gained by cold bodies} = \text{Heat lost by hot bodies}$$

## 4. Modes of Heat Transfer

### Conduction

<Formula label="Fourier's Law of Conduction">
$$\frac{dQ}{dt} = -kA\frac{dT}{dx}$$
</Formula>

$k$ = thermal conductivity (W/m·K). Negative sign: heat flows down the temperature gradient.

**Thermal resistance:** $R_{th} = L/(kA)$ (analogous to electrical resistance).

- Series: $R_{total} = R_1 + R_2$
- Parallel: $1/R_{total} = 1/R_1 + 1/R_2$

**Steady-state heat flow:** $H = \frac{kA(T_1-T_2)}{L}$

### Convection

Heat transfer by bulk fluid motion. **Natural** (buoyancy-driven) or **forced**.

### Radiation

All objects emit electromagnetic radiation. Power emitted by a black body:

<Formula label="Stefan–Boltzmann Law">
$$P = \sigma A T^4, \quad \sigma = 5.67\times10^{-8}\ \text{W m}^{-2}\text{K}^{-4}$$
</Formula>

For a grey body with emissivity $\varepsilon$ ($0 < \varepsilon \leq 1$):
$$P = \varepsilon\sigma A T^4$$

**Net radiation exchange** between body (temp $T$) and surroundings (temp $T_0$):
$$P_{net} = \varepsilon\sigma A(T^4 - T_0^4)$$

---

## 5. Wien's Displacement Law

$$\lambda_{max}\cdot T = b, \quad b = 2.898\times10^{-3}\ \text{m·K}$$

The wavelength of maximum emission is inversely proportional to absolute temperature.

---

## 6. Newton's Law of Cooling

For small temperature differences ($T - T_0$ small):

<Formula label="Newton's Law of Cooling">
$$\frac{dT}{dt} = -K(T - T_0)$$
</Formula>

$$T(t) = T_0 + (T_i - T_0)\,e^{-Kt}$$

where $T_i$ = initial temperature, $T_0$ = ambient temperature, $K$ = cooling constant.

This is an approximation of Stefan's law for small $(T-T_0)$.

---

## 7. Thermal Stress

When a rod is constrained (fixed ends) and heated by $\Delta T$, it develops internal stress:

$$\sigma_{thermal} = Y\alpha\Delta T$$

where $Y$ = Young's modulus.
