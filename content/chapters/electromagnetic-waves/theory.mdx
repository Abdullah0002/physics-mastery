## 1. Maxwell's Equations (Conceptual)

Maxwell unified electricity and magnetism and predicted electromagnetic waves:

1. **Gauss's Law (Electric):** $\nabla\cdot\vec{E} = \rho/\varepsilon_0$
2. **Gauss's Law (Magnetic):** $\nabla\cdot\vec{B} = 0$ (no magnetic monopoles)
3. **Faraday's Law:** $\nabla\times\vec{E} = -\partial\vec{B}/\partial t$
4. **Ampere–Maxwell Law:** $\nabla\times\vec{B} = \mu_0\vec{J} + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\partial\vec{E}/\partial t$

The key addition: **displacement current** $I_d = \varepsilon_0\,d\Phi_E/dt$ (Maxwell's contribution).

---

## 2. Electromagnetic Waves

Maxwell predicted waves of oscillating $\vec{E}$ and $\vec{B}$ fields, mutually perpendicular and perpendicular to direction of propagation.

<Formula label="Speed of Light in vacuum">
$$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0}} = 3\times10^8\ \text{m/s}$$
</Formula>

**In a medium:** $v = c/n$ where $n = \sqrt{K\mu_r}$ = refractive index.

---

## 3. Properties of EM Waves

1. Transverse waves — $\vec{E}$ and $\vec{B}$ perpendicular to propagation direction $\hat{k}$, and to each other.
2. Travel at speed $c$ in vacuum.
3. Do not require a medium.
4. $E_0/B_0 = c$ (ratio of field amplitudes = speed of light).
5. Carry energy, momentum, and information.

---

## 4. Energy and Intensity

**Energy density:**

$$u = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 + \frac{B^2}{2\mu_0} = \varepsilon_0 E^2 = \frac{B^2}{\mu_0}$$

(Electric and magnetic energy densities are equal.)

<Formula label="Intensity (average)">
$$I = \frac{1}{2}c\varepsilon_0 E_0^2 = \frac{c B_0^2}{2\mu_0} = \frac{E_0 B_0}{2\mu_0}$$
</Formula>

**Radiation pressure:**

$$P_{rad} = \frac{I}{c} \quad \text{(perfectly absorbing)} \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{2I}{c} \quad \text{(perfectly reflecting)}$$

## 5. The Electromagnetic Spectrum

| Name | Wavelength range | How produced |
|---|---|---|
| Radio waves | $> 0.1\ \text{m}$ | Oscillating circuits, antennas |
| Microwaves | $1\ \text{mm} – 0.1\ \text{m}$ | Klystron/magnetron, molecular rotation |
| Infrared | $700\ \text{nm} – 1\ \text{mm}$ | Hot bodies, molecular vibrations |
| Visible light | $400 – 700\ \text{nm}$ | Electron transitions |
| Ultraviolet | $10 – 400\ \text{nm}$ | Hot stars, discharge lamps, electron transitions |
| X-rays | $0.01 – 10\ \text{nm}$ | High-energy electron bombardment of metals |
| Gamma rays | $< 0.01\ \text{nm}$ | Nuclear transitions, radioactive decay |

<Callout type="important">
The visible spectrum from violet to red: **VIBGYOR** (400 nm violet → 700 nm red). Violet has the highest frequency and shortest wavelength in visible; red has the lowest.
</Callout>

## 6. Wave Equations

$$E_y = E_0\sin(kx - \omega t), \quad B_z = B_0\sin(kx - \omega t)$$

$$E_0 = cB_0$$

---

## 7. Momentum

EM waves carry momentum. Momentum of photon:

$$p = \frac{E}{c} = \frac{h\nu}{c} = \frac{h}{\lambda}$$
