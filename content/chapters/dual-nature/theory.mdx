## 1. Wave-Particle Duality

Light and matter exhibit **both** wave-like and particle-like properties depending on the experiment:

| Phenomenon | Nature demonstrated |
|---|---|
| Interference, diffraction, polarization | Wave nature of light |
| Photoelectric effect, Compton effect | Particle (photon) nature of light |
| Electron diffraction (Davisson-Germer) | Wave nature of matter |
| Photoelectric effect / tracks in detector | Particle nature of matter |

## 2. Photoelectric Effect (Key Results)

<Formula label="Maximum Kinetic Energy">
$$KE_{max} = h\nu - \phi = eV_s$$
</Formula>

| Effect | Depends on |
|---|---|
| Emission of photoelectrons | Frequency $\geq \nu_0$ |
| Number of photoelectrons | Intensity |
| $KE_{max}$ | Frequency (not intensity) |
| Stopping potential $V_s$ | Frequency (not intensity) |

## 3. de Broglie Hypothesis

**Louis de Broglie (1924):** Any moving particle has an associated wavelength:

<Formula label="de Broglie Wavelength">
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
</Formula>

For electron accelerated through potential $V$:

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2m_e eV}} = \frac{1.226}{\sqrt{V}}\ \text{nm} \quad (V \text{ in volts})$$

<Callout type="important">
The de Broglie wavelength is significant (comparable to atomic/nuclear dimensions) only for microscopic particles. For macroscopic objects ($m \sim 1$ kg, $v \sim 1$ m/s), $\lambda \sim 10^{-34}$ m — completely unobservable.
</Callout>

---

## 4. Davisson-Germer Experiment (1927)

**Confirmed:** de Broglie's hypothesis.

Electrons were diffracted by a nickel crystal — they produced an interference pattern, demonstrating wave nature. The observed wavelength matched $\lambda = h/mv$.

---

## 5. Thomson's Experiment

G. P. Thomson independently confirmed electron diffraction by passing electrons through thin gold foil — produced diffraction rings like X-rays.

---

## 6. Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle (Review)

$$\Delta x\cdot\Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}, \quad \hbar = \frac{h}{2\pi}$$

The wave nature of particles means position and momentum cannot be simultaneously known with perfect precision.

---

## 7. Photon Properties

| Property | Expression |
|---|---|
| Energy | $E = h\nu = hc/\lambda$ |
| Momentum | $p = h/\lambda = E/c$ |
| Rest mass | 0 |
| Effective mass | $h\nu/c^2 = h/(c\lambda)$ |

---

## 8. Practical Applications

| Application | Principle |
|---|---|
| Photoelectric cell / solar cell | Photoelectric effect |
| Electron microscope | Wave nature of electrons (smaller $\lambda$ → higher resolution) |
| X-ray crystallography | Wave nature of X-rays/electrons |
| Photomultiplier tube | Photoelectric effect |
| LED | Recombination of electrons and holes, photon emission |
