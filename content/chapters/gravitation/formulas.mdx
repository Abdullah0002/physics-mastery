## Newton's Law of Gravitation

<Formula label="Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation">
$$F = \frac{Gm_1 m_2}{r^2}, \quad G = 6.674\times10^{-11}\ \text{N m}^2\text{ kg}^{-2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Gravitational Field Strength">
$$\vec{g} = -\frac{GM}{r^2}\hat{r}, \quad F = m\vec{g}$$
</Formula>

---

## Acceleration due to Gravity

<Formula label="Surface Value of g">
$$g = \frac{GM_E}{R_E^2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Variation with Altitude h (h << R)">
$$g_h = g\left(1 - \frac{2h}{R}\right) \approx g - \frac{2gh}{R}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Variation with Altitude h (general)">
$$g_h = \frac{g}{\left(1 + h/R\right)^2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Variation with Depth d">
$$g_d = g\left(1 - \frac{d}{R}\right)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Variation with Latitude φ (Earth's rotation)">
$$g_\phi = g - \omega^2 R\cos^2\phi$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="tip">
$g$ is maximum at poles ($\phi = 90°$) and minimum at equator ($\phi = 0°$). Also, $g$ decreases both above and below the surface, but is zero only at the center.
</Callout>

## Gravitational Potential Energy

<Formula label="Gravitational Potential Energy">
$$U = -\frac{GMm}{r} \quad \text{(zero at infinity)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Gravitational Potential">
$$V = -\frac{GM}{r}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Gravitational Field from Potential">
$$g = -\frac{dV}{dr}$$
</Formula>

---

## Potential and Field — Shell and Sphere

| Region | Hollow Shell | Solid Sphere |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Outside ($r > R$) | $V = -GM/r$, $g = GM/r^2$ | Same |
| On surface ($r = R$) | $V = -GM/R$, $g = GM/R^2$ | Same |
| Inside ($r < R$) | $V = -GM/R$ (const), $g = 0$ | $V = -\frac{GM}{2R^3}(3R^2-r^2)$, $g = \frac{GMr}{R^3}$ |

## Kepler's Laws

<Formula label="Kepler's First Law (Ellipse)">
$$\text{Planets move in elliptical orbits with Sun at one focus}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Kepler's Second Law (Equal Areas)">
$$\frac{dA}{dt} = \frac{L}{2m} = \text{constant}$$
$$\text{(Conservation of angular momentum)}$$
$$r_p v_p = r_a v_a$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Kepler's Third Law (Harmonic Law)">
$$T^2 \propto a^3, \quad \frac{T^2}{a^3} = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$$
$$\text{(a = semi-major axis)}$$
</Formula>

## Orbital Mechanics

<Formula label="Orbital Speed (Circular Orbit)">
$$v_o = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} = \sqrt{\frac{gR^2}{r}}$$
$$v_o = \sqrt{gR} \approx 7.9\ \text{km s}^{-1} \quad \text{(surface orbit)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Orbital Period">
$$T = \frac{2\pi r}{v_o} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{r^3}{GM}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Orbital Energy">
$$E = KE + U = \frac{GMm}{2r} - \frac{GMm}{r} = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$
$$KE = \frac{GMm}{2r} = -\frac{E}{1}, \quad U = -\frac{GMm}{r} = 2E$$
</Formula>

---

## Escape Velocity

<Formula label="Escape Velocity">
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}} = \sqrt{2gR} \approx 11.2\ \text{km s}^{-1}\ \text{(Earth)}$$
$$v_e = \sqrt{2}\,v_o \quad \text{(for surface orbit)}$$
</Formula>

---

## Satellite Dynamics

<Formula label="Height of Geostationary Orbit">
$$T = 24\ \text{h} \implies r = \left(\frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}\right)^{1/3} \approx 36000\ \text{km above surface}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Binding Energy of Satellite">
$$BE = -E = \frac{GMm}{2r}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Energy to Lift Satellite from Surface to Orbit r">
$$\Delta E = -\frac{GMm}{2r} - \left(-\frac{GMm}{R}\right) = GMm\left(\frac{1}{R} - \frac{1}{2r}\right)$$
</Formula>

## Two-Body Problem

<Formula label="Reduced Mass">
$$\mu = \frac{m_1 m_2}{m_1 + m_2}, \quad M = m_1 + m_2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Orbital Speed in Two-Body Problem">
$$v_1 = \sqrt{\frac{Gm_2^2}{(m_1+m_2)r}}, \quad v_2 = \sqrt{\frac{Gm_1^2}{(m_1+m_2)r}}$$
</Formula>
