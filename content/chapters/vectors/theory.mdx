## 1. Scalars and Vectors

**Scalar:** Magnitude only. Examples: mass, time, temperature, energy.

**Vector:** Magnitude + direction. Examples: displacement, velocity, acceleration, force.

---

## 2. Representation

A vector $\vec{A}$ is written with an arrow. Magnitude: $|\vec{A}|$ or $A$.

**Unit vector:** $\hat{A} = \vec{A}/|\vec{A}|$ — points in the direction of $\vec{A}$, magnitude = 1.

Standard basis: $\hat{i}$ (x-axis), $\hat{j}$ (y-axis), $\hat{k}$ (z-axis).

$$\vec{A} = A_x\hat{i} + A_y\hat{j} + A_z\hat{k}$$

$$|\vec{A}| = \sqrt{A_x^2 + A_y^2 + A_z^2}$$

---

## 3. Vector Addition

### Triangle / Parallelogram Law

$$\vec{R} = \vec{A} + \vec{B}$$

<Formula label="Magnitude of Resultant">
$$R = \sqrt{A^2 + B^2 + 2AB\cos\theta}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Direction of Resultant (angle with A)">
$$\tan\alpha = \frac{B\sin\theta}{A + B\cos\theta}$$
</Formula>

where $\theta$ is the angle between $\vec{A}$ and $\vec{B}$.

**Special cases:**
- $\theta = 0°$: $R = A+B$ (maximum)
- $\theta = 180°$: $R = |A-B|$ (minimum)
- $\theta = 90°$: $R = \sqrt{A^2+B^2}$
- $A = B$: $R = 2A\cos(\theta/2)$

<Callout type="tip">
For **two equal vectors** at angle $\theta$: resultant $= 2A\cos(\theta/2)$. This comes from the identity $\sqrt{2+2\cos\theta} = 2\cos(\theta/2)$.
</Callout>

## 4. Dot (Scalar) Product

<Formula label="Dot Product">
$$\vec{A}\cdot\vec{B} = AB\cos\theta = A_xB_x + A_yB_y + A_zB_z$$
</Formula>

**Properties:**
- Commutative: $\vec{A}\cdot\vec{B} = \vec{B}\cdot\vec{A}$
- Result is a **scalar**
- $\hat{i}\cdot\hat{i} = 1$, $\hat{i}\cdot\hat{j} = 0$
- $\vec{A}\cdot\vec{A} = A^2$

**Physical examples:** Work = $\vec{F}\cdot\vec{d}$; Power = $\vec{F}\cdot\vec{v}$.

---

## 5. Cross (Vector) Product

<Formula label="Cross Product Magnitude">
$$|\vec{A}\times\vec{B}| = AB\sin\theta$$
</Formula>

Direction: perpendicular to both $\vec{A}$ and $\vec{B}$ (right-hand rule).

$$\vec{A}\times\vec{B} = \begin{vmatrix}\hat{i}&\hat{j}&\hat{k}\\A_x&A_y&A_z\\B_x&B_y&B_z\end{vmatrix}$$

**Properties:**
- **Anti-commutative:** $\vec{A}\times\vec{B} = -\vec{B}\times\vec{A}$
- $\hat{i}\times\hat{j} = \hat{k}$, $\hat{j}\times\hat{k} = \hat{i}$, $\hat{k}\times\hat{i} = \hat{j}$
- $\vec{A}\times\vec{A} = \vec{0}$

**Physical examples:** Torque $= \vec{r}\times\vec{F}$; Angular momentum $= \vec{r}\times\vec{p}$.

<Callout type="important">
**Key test:** If $\vec{A}\cdot\vec{B} = |\vec{A}\times\vec{B}|$, then $\cos\theta = \sin\theta$, giving $\theta = 45°$.
</Callout>

---

## 6. Resolution of Vectors

Any vector in a plane can be resolved into two perpendicular components:

$$A_x = A\cos\theta, \qquad A_y = A\sin\theta$$

In 3D: project onto all three axes.

**Lami's Theorem:** If three concurrent coplanar forces $P$, $Q$, $R$ are in equilibrium, and the angles between them (opposite to each force) are $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$:

<Formula label="Lami's Theorem">
$$\frac{P}{\sin\alpha} = \frac{Q}{\sin\beta} = \frac{R}{\sin\gamma}$$
</Formula>

---

## 7. Position & Displacement Vectors

**Position vector** of point $(x, y, z)$: $\vec{r} = x\hat{i}+y\hat{j}+z\hat{k}$

**Displacement vector:** $\Delta\vec{r} = \vec{r}_2 - \vec{r}_1$

---

## 8. Conditions for Special Results

| Condition | Meaning |
|---|---|
| $\vec{A}\cdot\vec{B} = 0$ | $\vec{A}\perp\vec{B}$ (or one is zero) |
| $ \vert \vec{A}\times\vec{B} \vert  = 0$ | $\vec{A}\parallel\vec{B}$ (or one is zero) |
| $\vec{A} + \vec{B} = \vec{A} - \vec{B}$ | $\vec{B} = \vec{0}$ |
| $ \vert \vec{A}+\vec{B} \vert  =  \vert \vec{A}-\vec{B} \vert $ | $\vec{A}\perp\vec{B}$ |
| $ \vert \vec{A}+\vec{B} \vert  =  \vert \vec{A} \vert  =  \vert \vec{B} \vert $ | Angle between them is $120°$ |

---

## 9. Component Form Summary

| Operation | Result |
|---|---|
| $\vec{A}+\vec{B}$ | $(A_x+B_x)\hat{i}+(A_y+B_y)\hat{j}+(A_z+B_z)\hat{k}$ |
| $\vec{A}\cdot\vec{B}$ | $A_xB_x+A_yB_y+A_zB_z$ (scalar) |
| $\vec{A}\times\vec{B}$ | $(A_yB_z-A_zB_y)\hat{i}-(A_xB_z-A_zB_x)\hat{j}+(A_xB_y-A_yB_x)\hat{k}$ |

<Callout type="derivation">
The cross product determinant formula is the standard method. Expand along the first row. Be careful with the sign of the $\hat{j}$ term (negative).
</Callout>
