## Ideal Gas Law

<Formula label="Ideal Gas Equation">
$$PV = nRT = NkT$$
$$n = \text{moles},\quad N = \text{molecules},\quad R = 8.314\ \text{J mol}^{-1}\text{K}^{-1},\quad k_B = 1.38\times10^{-23}\ \text{J K}^{-1}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Combined Gas Law">
$$\frac{P_1 V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2 V_2}{T_2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures">
$$P_{\text{total}} = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 + \cdots$$
</Formula>

---

## Molecular Speeds

<Formula label="Most Probable Speed">
$$v_p = \sqrt{\frac{2RT}{M}} = \sqrt{\frac{2k_BT}{m}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Average Speed">
$$\bar{v} = \sqrt{\frac{8RT}{\pi M}} = \sqrt{\frac{8k_BT}{\pi m}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="RMS Speed">
$$v_{\text{rms}} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}} = \sqrt{\frac{3k_BT}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{3P}{\rho}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Speed Ratios">
$$v_p : \bar{v} : v_{\text{rms}} = \sqrt{2} : \sqrt{\frac{8}{\pi}} : \sqrt{3} \approx 1 : 1.128 : 1.225$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
$v_p < \bar{v} < v_{\text{rms}}$ always. For JEE, memorize the ratio $1 : 1.13 : 1.22$.
</Callout>

## Pressure and Kinetic Energy

<Formula label="Pressure from Kinetic Theory">
$$P = \frac{1}{3}\rho v_{\text{rms}}^2 = \frac{1}{3}\frac{mN}{V}v_{\text{rms}}^2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Average Translational KE per Molecule">
$$\langle KE \rangle = \frac{1}{2}mv_{\text{rms}}^2 = \frac{3}{2}k_BT$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Total Translational KE of Gas">
$$KE_{\text{total}} = \frac{3}{2}nRT = \frac{3}{2}Nk_BT$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Relation: Pressure and Kinetic Energy Density">
$$P = \frac{2}{3}\frac{KE_{\text{translational}}}{V}$$
</Formula>

## Equipartition of Energy

<Formula label="Equipartition Theorem">
$$\text{Energy per degree of freedom} = \frac{1}{2}k_BT$$
$$U_{\text{molecule}} = \frac{f}{2}k_BT, \quad U_{\text{total}} = \frac{f}{2}nRT$$
$$\text{(f = degrees of freedom)}$$
</Formula>

| Gas Type | f | Cv | Cp | γ |
|----------|---|----|----|---|
| Monatomic (He, Ar) | 3 | $\frac{3}{2}R$ | $\frac{5}{2}R$ | $5/3 \approx 1.67$ |
| Diatomic (H₂, O₂, N₂) at room T | 5 | $\frac{5}{2}R$ | $\frac{7}{2}R$ | $7/5 = 1.4$ |
| Diatomic (high T, vibrational) | 7 | $\frac{7}{2}R$ | $\frac{9}{2}R$ | $9/7 \approx 1.29$ |
| Polyatomic non-linear | 6 | $3R$ | $4R$ | $4/3 \approx 1.33$ |

## Specific Heats

<Formula label="Mayer's Relation">
$$C_p - C_v = R$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Ratio of Specific Heats">
$$\gamma = \frac{C_p}{C_v} = 1 + \frac{2}{f}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Internal Energy of Ideal Gas">
$$U = nC_vT = \frac{f}{2}nRT$$
$$\Delta U = nC_v\Delta T \quad \text{(valid for any process)}$$
</Formula>

---

## Mean Free Path

<Formula label="Mean Free Path">
$$\lambda = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}\pi d^2 n}$$
$$\text{(d = diameter of molecule, n = number density N/V)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Mean Free Path in Terms of P, T">
$$\lambda = \frac{k_BT}{\sqrt{2}\pi d^2 P}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Collision Frequency">
$$Z = \frac{\bar{v}}{\lambda} = \sqrt{2}\pi d^2 n \bar{v}$$
</Formula>

---

## Graham's Law of Effusion

<Formula label="Graham's Law">
$$\frac{r_1}{r_2} = \sqrt{\frac{M_2}{M_1}} = \sqrt{\frac{\rho_2}{\rho_1}} \quad \text{(at same T and P)}$$
</Formula>

---

## Van der Waals Equation (Real Gases)

<Formula label="Van der Waals Equation">
$$\left(P + \frac{an^2}{V^2}\right)(V - nb) = nRT$$
$$\text{a = intermolecular attraction constant; b = excluded volume per mole}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Boyle Temperature">
$$T_B = \frac{a}{Rb}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Critical Constants">
$$T_c = \frac{8a}{27Rb}, \quad V_c = 3nb, \quad P_c = \frac{a}{27b^2}$$
</Formula>
