## 1. Nature of Sound

Sound is a **longitudinal mechanical wave** — particles oscillate parallel to propagation direction. Requires a medium.

**Speed of sound in a medium:**

<Formula label="Speed of sound (general)">
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{B}{\rho}}$$
</Formula>

where $B$ = bulk modulus, $\rho$ = density.

---

## 2. Speed of Sound in Air

Newton assumed isothermal: $v = \sqrt{P/\rho}$. This underestimates.

**Laplace's correction** (adiabatic propagation):

<Formula label="Laplace's Formula">
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma P}{\rho}} = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma RT}{M}}$$
</Formula>

At 0°C in air: $v \approx 331\ \text{m/s}$.

**Effect of temperature:** $v \propto \sqrt{T}$

$$v_T \approx 331 + 0.61\,T_{°C} \quad (\text{m/s})$$

**Effect of humidity:** Humid air is less dense → sound travels faster in humid air.

## 3. Organ Pipes

### Open Pipe (both ends open)

Both ends are **antinodes**. Standing wave condition:

$$L = n\frac{\lambda}{2}, \quad f_n = \frac{nv}{2L}, \quad n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$$

All harmonics (fundamental + even + odd overtones) are present.

<Formula label="Fundamental — Open Pipe">
$$f_1 = \frac{v}{2L}$$
</Formula>

### Closed Pipe (one end closed)

Closed end = **node**, open end = **antinode**.

$$L = (2n-1)\frac{\lambda}{4}, \quad f_n = \frac{(2n-1)v}{4L}, \quad n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$$

Only **odd harmonics** present: $f_1, 3f_1, 5f_1, \ldots$

<Formula label="Fundamental — Closed Pipe">
$$f_1 = \frac{v}{4L}$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
Fundamental of **open pipe = twice** that of closed pipe of same length. Open pipe has richer tone (more harmonics).
</Callout>

### End Correction

Real pipes have an antinode slightly beyond the open end. End correction $e \approx 0.6r$ (r = radius).

Effective length: $L_{eff} = L + e$ (closed: $+e$; open: $+2e$).

---

## 4. Resonance with a Tuning Fork

First resonance: $L_1 = \lambda/4$; second resonance: $L_2 = 3\lambda/4$.

$$\lambda = 2(L_2 - L_1), \quad v = f\lambda = 2f(L_2 - L_1)$$

---

## 5. Beats

Two sound sources with frequencies $f_1$ and $f_2$ ($|f_1-f_2|$ small):

$$f_{beat} = |f_1 - f_2|$$

Each beat = one loudness maximum per second.

**Application:** Tuning instruments — when beat frequency → 0, the two sources are in tune.

---

## 6. Sound Intensity

<Formula label="Intensity">
$$I = \frac{P}{4\pi r^2} \quad \text{(point source, W/m}^2\text{)}$$
</Formula>

**Intensity level (decibels):**

$$\beta = 10\log_{10}\frac{I}{I_0}, \quad I_0 = 10^{-12}\ \text{W/m}^2$$

$I \propto A^2$: doubling amplitude quadruples intensity (+6 dB).

---

## 7. Doppler Effect (Sound)

(See Waves chapter for full formula.)

<Formula label="Doppler (sound)">
$$f' = f\cdot\frac{v + v_O}{v - v_S}$$
</Formula>

**Sonic boom / Mach number:**

$$M = \frac{v_{source}}{v_{sound}}$$

When $M > 1$ (supersonic), shock waves form.

---

## 8. Reflection and Echo

Minimum distance to hear a distinct echo: $d = vt/2$ where $t \geq 0.1\ \text{s}$ (human persistence of hearing).

$$d_{min} = \frac{331\times0.1}{2} \approx 16.5\ \text{m}$$

## 9. Summary

| Quantity | Formula |
|---|---|
| Speed in air (Laplace) | $\sqrt{\gamma RT/M}$ |
| Open pipe fundamental | $v/(2L)$ |
| Closed pipe fundamental | $v/(4L)$ |
| Beat frequency | $\ \vert f_1 - f_2\ \vert $ |
| Intensity level | $10\log(I/I_0)$ dB |
| Doppler | $f(v+v_O)/(v-v_S)$ |
