## 1. Energy Bands in Solids

| Material | Band gap | Conductivity |
|---|---|---|
| Conductor (metal) | Overlapping bands or half-filled | High |
| Semiconductor (Si, Ge) | Small gap (~1 eV) | Intermediate |
| Insulator (diamond) | Large gap (~5 eV) | Very low |

- **Valence band:** Fully occupied at 0 K.
- **Conduction band:** Empty at 0 K; carries current when electrons are excited.
- **Fermi level:** Energy level with 50% probability of occupation.

---

## 2. Intrinsic Semiconductors

Pure semiconductor. At room temperature, thermal excitation creates electron-hole pairs.

$$n_e = n_h = n_i \quad \text{(intrinsic carrier density)}$$

Both electrons (in conduction band) and holes (in valence band) conduct.

---

## 3. Extrinsic Semiconductors (Doping)

Adding impurities (dopants) creates extra carriers.

### n-type

Donor impurity (Group V: P, As, Sb). Extra electron available.

$$n_e \gg n_h, \quad \text{majority carriers: electrons}$$

### p-type

Acceptor impurity (Group III: B, Al, Ga). Creates holes.

$$n_h \gg n_e, \quad \text{majority carriers: holes}$$

**Mass action law:**

$$n_e \cdot n_h = n_i^2 \quad \text{(constant at fixed temperature)}$$

---

## 4. p-n Junction

When p-type and n-type are joined:
- Diffusion of electrons to p-side, holes to n-side.
- Creates **depletion region** (no free carriers).
- Built-in potential $V_0$ (contact potential): ~0.7 V for Si, ~0.3 V for Ge.

---

## 5. Biasing a p-n Junction

### Forward Bias

Positive to p-side, negative to n-side. Reduces potential barrier → current flows easily above threshold (~0.7 V for Si).

### Reverse Bias

Positive to n-side, negative to p-side. Increases potential barrier → only tiny reverse saturation current $I_0$.

**At breakdown** (Zener or avalanche): large current at fixed reverse voltage — used in Zener diode for voltage regulation.

---

## 6. Junction Diode — Ideal Characteristics

**Diode equation:** $I = I_0(e^{eV/k_BT} - 1)$

Forward bias: exponential increase. Reverse bias: $I \approx -I_0$ (very small).

---

## 7. Rectifier Circuits

**Half-wave rectifier:** Single diode — passes only positive half cycles.

$$V_{dc} = V_m/\pi, \quad \text{ripple factor} = 1.21$$

**Full-wave rectifier (bridge):** 4 diodes — passes both half cycles.

$$V_{dc} = 2V_m/\pi, \quad \text{ripple factor} = 0.482$$

---

## 8. Zener Diode

Works in reverse breakdown (Zener region). Maintains constant voltage across load.

Used for **voltage regulation**.

---

## 9. Transistor (BJT)

Three terminals: Emitter (E), Base (B), Collector (C). Two types: npn and pnp.

**Modes of operation:** Cutoff (off), Saturation (on), Active (amplifier).

**In active mode:**

$$I_E = I_C + I_B, \quad \beta = \frac{I_C}{I_B} \quad (\text{current gain, typically 50–300})$$

$$\alpha = \frac{I_C}{I_E} = \frac{\beta}{\beta+1}$$

**Transistor as switch:** In cutoff: OFF ($I_C \approx 0$). In saturation: ON ($V_{CE} \approx 0$).

**Transistor as amplifier (CE configuration):**

$$A_V = \frac{\Delta V_C}{\Delta V_B} = -\beta\frac{R_C}{R_i} \quad (\text{voltage gain, inverted})$$

---

## 10. Logic Gates

| Gate | Symbol | Output |
|---|---|---|
| AND | $A\cdot B$ | 1 only if both inputs 1 |
| OR | $A+B$ | 1 if at least one input 1 |
| NOT | $\bar{A}$ | Output inverted |
| NAND | $\overline{A\cdot B}$ | AND then NOT (universal gate) |
| NOR | $\overline{A+B}$ | OR then NOT (universal gate) |
| XOR | $A\oplus B$ | 1 if inputs differ |

<Callout type="important">
NAND and NOR are **universal gates** — any logic function can be built from NAND alone or NOR alone.
</Callout>

## 11. Optoelectronic Devices

| Device | Principle |
|---|---|
| LED (Light Emitting Diode) | Forward-biased junction; recombination emits photons |
| Photodiode | Reverse-biased; photons generate electron-hole pairs → current |
| Solar cell | Photovoltaic: photons create current, no external bias needed |
| Phototransistor | Light controls base current |

## 12. Summary

| Quantity | Formula |
|---|---|
| Mass action law | $n_e n_h = n_i^2$ |
| Transistor $\beta$ | $I_C/I_B$ |
| $\alpha$–$\beta$ relation | $\beta = \alpha/(1-\alpha)$ |
| Full-wave $V_{dc}$ | $2V_m/\pi$ |
| CE voltage gain | $\beta R_C/R_i$ |
