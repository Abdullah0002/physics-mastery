## 1. Types of Waves

- **Transverse:** Particle displacement perpendicular to wave propagation (e.g., light, string waves).
- **Longitudinal:** Particle displacement parallel to wave propagation (e.g., sound, compression waves).

---

## 2. Wave Parameters

| Parameter | Symbol | Relation |
|---|---|---|
| Wavelength | $\lambda$ | distance between successive identical points |
| Frequency | $f$ | oscillations per second |
| Time period | $T$ | $1/f$ |
| Wave speed | $v$ | $v = f\lambda = \lambda/T$ |
| Angular frequency | $\omega$ | $2\pi f$ |
| Wave number | $k$ | $2\pi/\lambda = \omega/v$ |

---

## 3. Wave Equation

For a wave traveling in the +$x$ direction:

<Formula label="Progressive Wave">
$$y(x,t) = A\sin(kx - \omega t + \phi_0) = A\sin\left(\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}x - \frac{2\pi}{T}t\right)$$
</Formula>

- $A$ = amplitude
- Particle velocity: $v_p = \partial y/\partial t = -A\omega\cos(kx-\omega t)$
- Particle acceleration: $a_p = -A\omega^2\sin(kx-\omega t) = -\omega^2 y$

**Speed of a wave on string:**

<Formula label="Wave speed on string">
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$$
</Formula>

where $T$ = tension, $\mu$ = mass per unit length.

---

## 4. Energy and Intensity

Energy per unit length: $\frac{1}{2}\mu\omega^2 A^2$ (for string wave).

**Intensity:** $I \propto A^2 \propto 1/r^2$ (for spherical wave in 3D).

---

## 5. Principle of Superposition

When two or more waves overlap, the net displacement is the **algebraic sum** of individual displacements.

$$y = y_1 + y_2$$

---

## 6. Standing Waves

Two identical waves traveling in opposite directions:

$$y_1 = A\sin(kx-\omega t), \quad y_2 = A\sin(kx+\omega t)$$

$$y = y_1+y_2 = 2A\sin(kx)\cos(\omega t)$$

- **Nodes:** $2A\sin(kx)=0 \Rightarrow kx = n\pi \Rightarrow x = n\lambda/2$
- **Antinodes:** $|2A\sin(kx)| = 2A \Rightarrow kx = (2n+1)\pi/2 \Rightarrow x = (2n+1)\lambda/4$

---

## 7. Standing Waves on a String

**Both ends fixed** (string/open pipe analogy):

$$L = n\frac{\lambda}{2} \Rightarrow \lambda_n = \frac{2L}{n}, \quad f_n = \frac{nv}{2L}$$

$n = 1$ is the **fundamental** (first harmonic). All harmonics present.

<Formula label="Fundamental frequency (string)">
$$f_1 = \frac{1}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$$
</Formula>

---

## 8. Beats

Two waves of slightly different frequencies $f_1$ and $f_2$:

<Formula label="Beat Frequency">
$$f_{beat} = |f_1 - f_2|$$
</Formula>

The amplitude modulates at $f_{beat}$: loudness oscillates $f_{beat}$ times per second.

---

## 9. Doppler Effect (Sound)

Apparent frequency when source and observer are in relative motion:

<Formula label="Doppler Effect">
$$f' = f\cdot\frac{v + v_O}{v - v_S}$$
</Formula>

**Sign convention (most common form):**
- $v_O$ is **+ve** when observer moves **toward** source.
- $v_S$ is **+ve** when source moves **toward** observer.
- $v$ = speed of sound in medium.

| Situation | Result |
|---|---|
| Source and observer approaching | $f' > f$ (blue shift) |
| Source and observer receding | $f' < f$ (red shift) |
| Observer moves toward stationary source | $f' = f(v+v_O)/v$ |
| Source moves toward stationary observer | $f' = fv/(v-v_S)$ |

<Callout type="warning">
Doppler effect depends on **relative motion** but is **not symmetric** in sound — it matters whether the source or observer is moving. In light (special relativity), it is symmetric.
</Callout>

---

## 10. Reflection at Boundaries

- **Fixed end (rigid boundary):** Wave reflected with **phase change of $\pi$** (crest → trough). Reflected wave: $-A$.
- **Free end (loose boundary):** Wave reflected **without** phase change.

---

## 11. Resonance in Strings

Resonance occurs when the string length accommodates a whole number of half-wavelengths:

$$f_n = \frac{n}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}, \quad n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$$

---

## 12. Summary

| Quantity | Formula |
|---|---|
| Wave speed | $v = f\lambda$ |
| Speed on string | $\sqrt{T/\mu}$ |
| Standing wave | $y = 2A\sin(kx)\cos(\omega t)$ |
| Fundamental (fixed ends) | $f_1 = v/(2L)$ |
| Beat frequency | $ \vert f_1 - f_2 \vert $ |
| Doppler (toward) | $f' = f(v+v_O)/(v-v_S)$ |
