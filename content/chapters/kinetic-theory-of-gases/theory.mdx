## 1. Ideal Gas Law

<Formula label="Ideal Gas Law">
$$PV = nRT = Nk_BT$$
</Formula>

- $n$ = number of moles, $R = 8.314\ \text{J mol}^{-1}\text{K}^{-1}$
- $N$ = number of molecules, $k_B = 1.38\times10^{-23}\ \text{J K}^{-1}$

**Avogadro's number:** $N_A = 6.022\times10^{23}\ \text{mol}^{-1}$; $k_B = R/N_A$.

---

## 2. Assumptions of Kinetic Theory

1. Gas consists of a large number of identical, point-sized molecules.
2. Molecules are in constant random motion; no preferred direction.
3. Collisions between molecules and with walls are **perfectly elastic**.
4. No intermolecular forces except during collisions (instantaneous).
5. Average kinetic energy is proportional to absolute temperature.

---

## 3. Pressure from Kinetic Theory

<Formula label="Pressure of Ideal Gas">
$$P = \frac{1}{3}\rho\overline{v^2} = \frac{1}{3}\frac{mN}{V}\overline{v^2}$$
</Formula>

where $\overline{v^2}$ = mean square speed.

---

## 4. Molecular Speeds

| Speed | Formula | Numerical ratio |
|---|---|---|
| RMS speed | $v_{rms} = \sqrt{3RT/M} = \sqrt{3k_BT/m}$ | 1.732 |
| Mean speed | $\bar{v} = \sqrt{8RT/\pi M}$ | 1.596 |
| Most probable speed | $v_p = \sqrt{2RT/M}$ | 1.414 |

(All proportional to $\sqrt{T/M}$.)

<Callout type="important">
$v_p < \bar{v} < v_{rms}$. The ratio is $v_p : \bar{v} : v_{rms} = \sqrt{2} : \sqrt{8/\pi} : \sqrt{3} \approx 1 : 1.13 : 1.22$.
</Callout>

---

## 5. Kinetic Energy and Temperature

<Formula label="Average KE per molecule">
$$\overline{KE} = \frac{1}{2}m\overline{v^2} = \frac{3}{2}k_BT$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Total internal energy (monatomic, N molecules)">
$$U = N\cdot\frac{3}{2}k_BT = \frac{3}{2}nRT$$
</Formula>

---

## 6. Degrees of Freedom (DoF)

| Gas type | Translational | Rotational | Vibrational | Total $f$ (at room temp) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Monatomic (He, Ar) | 3 | 0 | 0 | 3 |
| Diatomic (N₂, O₂) | 3 | 2 | 0 | 5 |
| Polyatomic (non-linear, NH₃) | 3 | 3 | 0 | 6 |

**Vibrational modes** contribute at high temperatures only (each vibrational mode = 2 DoF: KE + PE).

---

## 7. Law of Equipartition of Energy

Each degree of freedom contributes $\frac{1}{2}k_BT$ of energy per molecule.

<Formula label="Average energy per molecule">
$$\overline{E} = \frac{f}{2}k_BT$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Total internal energy">
$$U = \frac{f}{2}nRT$$
</Formula>

---

## 8. Specific Heats of Gases

Using equipartition:

<Formula label="Molar heat capacity at constant volume">
$$C_V = \frac{f}{2}R$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Molar heat capacity at constant pressure">
$$C_P = C_V + R = \frac{f+2}{2}R$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Ratio of heat capacities">
$$\gamma = \frac{C_P}{C_V} = \frac{f+2}{f}$$
</Formula>

| Gas type | $f$ | $C_V$ | $C_P$ | $\gamma$ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Monatomic | 3 | $\frac{3}{2}R$ | $\frac{5}{2}R$ | 5/3 |
| Diatomic | 5 | $\frac{5}{2}R$ | $\frac{7}{2}R$ | 7/5 |
| Polyatomic | 6 | $3R$ | $4R$ | 4/3 |

---

## 9. Mean Free Path

Average distance a molecule travels between successive collisions:

<Formula label="Mean Free Path">
$$\lambda = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}\pi d^2 n}$$
</Formula>

where $d$ = molecular diameter, $n = N/V$ = number density.

Using ideal gas: $\lambda = \frac{k_BT}{\sqrt{2}\pi d^2 P}$.

---

## 10. Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures

For a mixture of ideal gases:

$$P_{total} = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 + \cdots$$

Each gas exerts pressure independently.

---

## 11. Graham's Law of Diffusion

$$\frac{r_1}{r_2} = \sqrt{\frac{M_2}{M_1}} = \frac{v_{rms,1}}{v_{rms,2}}$$

Lighter gas diffuses faster.
