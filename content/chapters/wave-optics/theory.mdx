## 1. Huygens' Principle

Every point on a wavefront is the source of secondary spherical wavelets. The new wavefront is the forward tangent to all secondary wavelets.

Used to derive: reflection, refraction, and the laws of wave propagation.

---

## 2. Coherence

**Coherent sources:** Same frequency, constant phase difference. Required for sustained interference.

**Incoherent sources:** Random phase difference — no permanent interference pattern.

---

## 3. Principle of Superposition

The net displacement at any point = algebraic sum of individual displacements.

**Constructive interference:** Waves in phase → amplitude = $A_1 + A_2$

**Destructive interference:** Waves exactly out of phase ($\pi$) → amplitude = $|A_1 - A_2|$

---

## 4. Young's Double Slit Experiment (YDSE)

Two coherent slits $S_1$ and $S_2$ separated by $d$, at distance $D$ from screen.

**Path difference:** $\Delta = d\sin\theta \approx \frac{dy}{D}$ for small $\theta$.

**Condition for bright fringe (constructive):** $\Delta = n\lambda$

**Condition for dark fringe (destructive):** $\Delta = (2n+1)\lambda/2$

<Formula label="Fringe width">
$$\beta = \frac{\lambda D}{d}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Position of nth bright fringe">
$$y_n = \frac{n\lambda D}{d}$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
Fringe width $\beta$ is the same for all fringes (bright and dark). The central bright fringe is at $y=0$.
</Callout>

### Effect of Thin Film / Medium

If a thin transparent sheet (refractive index $n$, thickness $t$) is placed over one slit:

**Extra path length introduced:** $(n-1)t$

**Shift of central fringe toward the side of the slab:**

$$y_{shift} = \frac{(n-1)t\cdot D}{d}$$

---

## 5. Intensity in YDSE

For two coherent sources of amplitude $A$:

$$I = 4I_0\cos^2\frac{\delta}{2}$$

where $\delta = 2\pi\Delta/\lambda$ = phase difference.

At maxima ($\delta = 2n\pi$): $I = 4I_0$. At minima ($\delta = (2n+1)\pi$): $I = 0$.

**Average intensity = $2I_0$** (energy conserved).

---

## 6. Single Slit Diffraction

For a slit of width $a$:

**First minimum:** $a\sin\theta = \lambda$

<Formula label="Angular width of central maximum">
$$2\theta \approx \frac{2\lambda}{a}$$
</Formula>

**Linear width of central maximum:** $w = 2\lambda D/a$

<Callout type="tip">
In diffraction, minima occur at $a\sin\theta = n\lambda$ ($n\neq 0$). In double-slit, maxima at $d\sin\theta = n\lambda$. Different conditions — don't confuse them.
</Callout>

---

## 7. Resolving Power

**Rayleigh criterion:** Two sources are just resolved when the central maximum of one falls on the first minimum of the other.

**Limiting angle:** $\theta_{min} = 1.22\lambda/D$ (for circular aperture of diameter $D$).

---

## 8. Polarization

Light is a transverse wave — $\vec{E}$ can oscillate in any direction perpendicular to propagation.

**Unpolarized light:** $\vec{E}$ oscillates randomly in all transverse directions.

**Plane polarized (linearly polarized):** $\vec{E}$ oscillates in one plane only.

### Malus's Law

When polarized light of intensity $I_0$ passes through a polarizer at angle $\theta$ to the polarization direction:

<Formula label="Malus's Law">
$$I = I_0\cos^2\theta$$
</Formula>

### Brewster's Law

When light is incident at Brewster's angle $\theta_B$, the reflected light is completely polarized:

<Formula label="Brewster's Angle">
$$\tan\theta_B = n$$
</Formula>

At Brewster's angle: reflected ray $\perp$ refracted ray.
