## Work

<Formula label="Work Done by Constant Force">
$$W = \vec{F}\cdot\vec{d} = Fd\cos\theta$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Work Done by Variable Force">
$$W = \int_{x_i}^{x_f} F\,dx = \int \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Work Done by Spring Force">
$$W_{\text{spring}} = -\frac{1}{2}kx^2 \quad \text{(from natural length)}$$
$$W = \frac{1}{2}k x_i^2 - \frac{1}{2}k x_f^2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Work Done by Gravity">
$$W_g = mgh \quad (\text{positive downward})$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="tip">
Work done by friction is always negative; work done by normal force and centripetal force is always zero.
</Callout>

---

## Work-Energy Theorem

<Formula label="Work-Energy Theorem">
$$W_{\text{net}} = \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Kinetic Energy">
$$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{p^2}{2m}$$
</Formula>

---

## Potential Energy

<Formula label="Gravitational Potential Energy">
$$U_g = mgh \quad (\text{near Earth's surface})$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Elastic Potential Energy (Spring)">
$$U_s = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Relation between Force and Potential Energy">
$$F = -\frac{dU}{dx}, \quad \vec{F} = -\nabla U$$
$$F_x = -\frac{\partial U}{\partial x},\ F_y = -\frac{\partial U}{\partial y},\ F_z = -\frac{\partial U}{\partial z}$$
</Formula>

---

## Conservation of Mechanical Energy

<Formula label="Conservation of Energy (conservative forces only)">
$$KE_i + U_i = KE_f + U_f$$
$$\Delta KE + \Delta U = 0$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Work done by Non-Conservative Forces">
$$W_{\text{nc}} = \Delta KE + \Delta U = \Delta E_{\text{mech}}$$
</Formula>

---

## Power

<Formula label="Average Power">
$$P_{\text{avg}} = \frac{W}{t}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Instantaneous Power">
$$P = \frac{dW}{dt} = \vec{F}\cdot\vec{v} = Fv\cos\theta$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Power of Engine">
$$P = Fv \quad \text{(when } F \parallel v\text{)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Efficiency">
$$\eta = \frac{P_{\text{output}}}{P_{\text{input}}} \times 100\%$$
</Formula>

## Collisions

<Formula label="Conservation of Momentum (always)">
$$m_1 u_1 + m_2 u_2 = m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Coefficient of Restitution">
$$e = \frac{v_2 - v_1}{u_1 - u_2} = \frac{\text{relative speed of separation}}{\text{relative speed of approach}}$$
</Formula>

| Type | e | KE |
|------|---|----|
| Perfectly elastic | 1 | Conserved |
| Partially elastic | 0 < e < 1 | Partially lost |
| Perfectly inelastic | 0 | Maximum loss |

<Formula label="Elastic Collision Velocities (1D)">
$$v_1 = \frac{(m_1-m_2)u_1 + 2m_2 u_2}{m_1+m_2}, \quad v_2 = \frac{(m_2-m_1)u_2 + 2m_1 u_1}{m_1+m_2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Perfectly Inelastic Collision">
$$v = \frac{m_1 u_1 + m_2 u_2}{m_1 + m_2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="KE Loss in Perfectly Inelastic Collision">
$$\Delta KE = \frac{m_1 m_2 (u_1 - u_2)^2}{2(m_1+m_2)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Elastic Collision: Equal Masses">
$$m_1 = m_2 \implies v_1 = u_2,\quad v_2 = u_1 \quad \text{(velocities exchanged)}$$
</Formula>

---

## Head-on Elastic Collision with Wall

<Formula label="Ball hitting massive wall (M → ∞)">
$$v_{\text{ball}} = -u_{\text{ball}} \quad \text{(speed unchanged, direction reversed)}$$
</Formula>

---

## Spring Potential Energy & Oscillations

<Formula label="Maximum Compression / Extension">
$$\frac{1}{2}kx^2 = \frac{1}{2}\mu v_{\text{rel}}^2, \quad \mu = \frac{m_1 m_2}{m_1+m_2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Velocities at Maximum Compression">
$$v_{\text{cm}} = \frac{m_1 u_1 + m_2 u_2}{m_1+m_2}\ (\text{both move at } v_{\text{cm}})$$
</Formula>
