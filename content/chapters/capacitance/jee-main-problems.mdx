## SECTION A — Capacitance Basics

### Problem 1
A conductor acquires a potential of $50\,\text V$ when given a charge of $1\,\mu\text C$. Its capacitance is:
**(A)** $20\,\text{nF}$ **(B)** $20\,\text{pF}$ **(C)** $50\,\text{nF}$ **(D)** $2\,\mu\text F$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$C=\dfrac{Q}{V}=\dfrac{1\times10^{-6}}{50}=2\times10^{-8}\,\text F=20\,\text{nF}.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 2
The radius of an isolated conducting sphere whose capacitance is $1\,\text{pF}$ is approximately:
**(A)** $9\,\text{mm}$ **(B)** $9\,\text{cm}$ **(C)** $9\,\text m$ **(D)** $0.9\,\text{mm}$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$C=4\pi\varepsilon_0R \Rightarrow R=\dfrac{C}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}=C\cdot k=(1\times10^{-12})(9\times10^9)=9\times10^{-3}\,\text m=9\,\text{mm}.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 3
A parallel-plate capacitor has plate area $A$ and separation $d$. If the area is tripled and separation doubled, the new capacitance is:
**(A)** $\tfrac32 C$ **(B)** $\tfrac23 C$ **(C)** $6C$ **(D)** $\tfrac16 C$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$C'=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0(3A)}{2d}=\dfrac32\cdot\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{d}=\dfrac32 C.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 4
Two isolated spheres of radii $R$ and $3R$ are charged to the same potential $V$. The ratio of charges on them is:
**(A)** $1:3$ **(B)** $3:1$ **(C)** $1:9$ **(D)** $9:1$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$Q=CV=4\pi\varepsilon_0RV$, so $Q\propto R$ at fixed $V$. Ratio $=R:3R=1:3.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

## SECTION B — Combinations of Capacitors

### Problem 5
Three capacitors $2,3,6\,\mu\text F$ are connected in series. The equivalent capacitance is:
**(A)** $1\,\mu\text F$ **(B)** $11\,\mu\text F$ **(C)** $\tfrac{6}{11}\,\mu\text F$ **(D)** $0.5\,\mu\text F$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$\dfrac{1}{C_{eq}}=\dfrac12+\dfrac13+\dfrac16=\dfrac{3+2+1}{6}=1 \Rightarrow C_{eq}=1\,\mu\text F.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 6
In Problem 5, the series combination is connected to $10\,\text V$. The charge on the $3\,\mu\text F$ capacitor is:
**(A)** $10\,\mu\text C$ **(B)** $30\,\mu\text C$ **(C)** $3.3\,\mu\text C$ **(D)** $5\,\mu\text C$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Series ⇒ same charge $Q=C_{eq}V=1\times10=10\,\mu\text C$ on **every** capacitor, including the $3\,\mu\text F$. **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 7
Two capacitors $4\,\mu\text F$ and $12\,\mu\text F$ are in parallel, then the combination is put in series with $3\,\mu\text F$. The equivalent capacitance is:
**(A)** $\tfrac{48}{19}\,\mu\text F$ **(B)** $2.5\,\mu\text F$ **(C)** $19\,\mu\text F$ **(D)** $\tfrac{16\cdot3}{19}\,\mu\text F$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Parallel: $4+12=16\,\mu\text F$. Series with $3$: $\dfrac{16\times3}{16+3}=\dfrac{48}{19}\approx2.53\,\mu\text F.$ **Answer: (A)** (= D, same value)
</details>

### Problem 8
$n$ identical capacitors each of capacitance $C$ give equivalent $C/n$ in one arrangement and $nC$ in another. The ratio (series : parallel) of equivalent capacitances is:
**(A)** $1:n^2$ **(B)** $1:n$ **(C)** $n^2:1$ **(D)** $1:1$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Series $=C/n$, parallel $=nC$. Ratio $=\dfrac{C/n}{nC}=\dfrac{1}{n^2}=1:n^2.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 9
Five capacitors of $2\,\mu\text F$ each: two in series form a branch, three in parallel form another branch, and the two branches are in parallel. The equivalent capacitance is:
**(A)** $7\,\mu\text F$ **(B)** $6\,\mu\text F$ **(C)** $8\,\mu\text F$ **(D)** $1\,\mu\text F$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Two $2\,\mu\text F$ in series $=1\,\mu\text F$. Three $2\,\mu\text F$ in parallel $=6\,\mu\text F$. Branches in parallel: $1+6=7\,\mu\text F.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 10
A $6\,\mu\text F$ and a $3\,\mu\text F$ capacitor are in series across $9\,\text V$. The potential difference across the $6\,\mu\text F$ is:
**(A)** $3\,\text V$ **(B)** $6\,\text V$ **(C)** $4.5\,\text V$ **(D)** $9\,\text V$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Voltage divides inversely with $C$: $V_6/V_3=C_3/C_6=3/6=1/2$, and $V_6+V_3=9$. So $V_6=3\,\text V$ (smaller voltage on bigger cap). **Answer: (A)**
</details>

## SECTION C — Energy Stored

### Problem 11
A $10\,\mu\text F$ capacitor is charged to $200\,\text V$. The energy stored is:
**(A)** $0.2\,\text J$ **(B)** $2\,\text J$ **(C)** $0.1\,\text J$ **(D)** $20\,\text J$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$U=\tfrac12CV^2=\tfrac12(10\times10^{-6})(200)^2=\tfrac12(10^{-5})(4\times10^4)=0.2\,\text J.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 12
The energy stored in a capacitor is $U$. If the charge is doubled (capacitance unchanged), the new energy is:
**(A)** $4U$ **(B)** $2U$ **(C)** $U/2$ **(D)** $U$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$U=Q^2/2C \Rightarrow U\propto Q^2$. Doubling $Q$ ⇒ $4U.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 13
A battery of EMF $V$ fully charges a capacitor $C$. The energy delivered by the battery and the energy stored in the capacitor are, respectively:
**(A)** $CV^2,\ \tfrac12CV^2$ **(B)** $\tfrac12CV^2,\ CV^2$ **(C)** $CV^2,\ CV^2$ **(D)** $\tfrac12CV^2,\ \tfrac12CV^2$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Battery moves $Q=CV$ through EMF $V$: work $=QV=CV^2$. Capacitor stores $\tfrac12CV^2$. (Half is lost.) **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 14
The electric field between two plates is $4\times10^5\,\text{V/m}$ in vacuum. The energy density is:
**(A)** $0.71\,\text{J/m}^3$ **(B)** $1.42\,\text{J/m}^3$ **(C)** $0.35\,\text{J/m}^3$ **(D)** $7.1\,\text{J/m}^3$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$u=\tfrac12\varepsilon_0E^2=\tfrac12(8.85\times10^{-12})(4\times10^5)^2=\tfrac12(8.85\times10^{-12})(1.6\times10^{11})=0.708\,\text{J/m}^3.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

## SECTION D — Dielectrics

### Problem 15
A parallel-plate capacitor of capacitance $C_0$ (air) is filled completely with a dielectric of constant $K=5$. The new capacitance is:
**(A)** $5C_0$ **(B)** $C_0/5$ **(C)** $C_0$ **(D)** $25C_0$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$C=KC_0=5C_0.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 16
A capacitor is charged by a battery to charge $Q$ and then the battery is **disconnected**. A dielectric $K$ is inserted. The new potential difference is:
**(A)** $V/K$ **(B)** $KV$ **(C)** $V$ **(D)** $V/K^2$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Battery off ⇒ $Q$ fixed. $V'=Q/C'=Q/(KC_0)=V/K.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 17
A capacitor remains connected to a battery of voltage $V$ while a dielectric $K$ is inserted. The charge becomes:
**(A)** $KQ_0$ **(B)** $Q_0/K$ **(C)** $Q_0$ **(D)** $K^2Q_0$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Battery on ⇒ $V$ fixed. $Q'=C'V=KC_0V=KQ_0.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 18
The free surface charge density on a capacitor plate is $\sigma_f$ and a dielectric $K=4$ fills the gap. The bound surface charge density is:
**(A)** $\tfrac34\sigma_f$ **(B)** $\tfrac14\sigma_f$ **(C)** $4\sigma_f$ **(D)** $\sigma_f$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$\sigma_b=\sigma_f(1-1/K)=\sigma_f(1-1/4)=\tfrac34\sigma_f.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 19
A dielectric slab of constant $K=2$ and thickness $d/2$ is inserted in a capacitor of plate gap $d$ (slab parallel to plates). The capacitance becomes:
**(A)** $\tfrac43 C_0$ **(B)** $2C_0$ **(C)** $\tfrac32 C_0$ **(D)** $C_0$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$C=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d-t(1-1/K)}=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d-\tfrac{d}{2}(1-\tfrac12)}=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d-\tfrac{d}{4}}=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{\tfrac34 d}=\tfrac43 C_0.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 20
Two dielectric slabs $K_1=2$ and $K_2=4$, each of thickness $d/2$, fill a capacitor of gap $d$ (stacked). The effective dielectric constant is:
**(A)** $\tfrac{8}{3}$ **(B)** $3$ **(C)** $6$ **(D)** $\tfrac{3}{8}$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Stacked = series: $C=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0 A}{\tfrac{d/2}{K_1}+\tfrac{d/2}{K_2}}=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{\tfrac{d}{2}(\tfrac12+\tfrac14)}=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{\tfrac{d}{2}\cdot\tfrac34}=\dfrac{8}{3}\cdot\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{d}.$ So $K_{eff}=\dfrac{2K_1K_2}{K_1+K_2}=\dfrac{2\cdot8}{6}=\tfrac83.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

## SECTION E — Charge Sharing & Circuits

### Problem 21
A $2\,\mu\text F$ capacitor at $100\,\text V$ is connected to an uncharged $3\,\mu\text F$. The common potential is:
**(A)** $40\,\text V$ **(B)** $50\,\text V$ **(C)** $60\,\text V$ **(D)** $100\,\text V$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$V=\dfrac{C_1V_1+C_2V_2}{C_1+C_2}=\dfrac{2\times100+3\times0}{5}=40\,\text V.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 22
In Problem 21, the energy lost in the process is:
**(A)** $6\,\text{mJ}$ **(B)** $10\,\text{mJ}$ **(C)** $4\,\text{mJ}$ **(D)** $0$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$\Delta U=\dfrac{C_1C_2(V_1-V_2)^2}{2(C_1+C_2)}=\dfrac{(2)(3)(100)^2}{2(5)}\,\mu\text J=\dfrac{6\times10^4}{10}=6000\,\mu\text J=6\,\text{mJ}.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 23
Two identical capacitors $C$ are charged to $V$ and $2V$ and connected positive-to-positive. The common potential is:
**(A)** $1.5V$ **(B)** $3V$ **(C)** $V$ **(D)** $0.5V$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$V_{common}=\dfrac{CV+C(2V)}{2C}=\dfrac{3CV}{2C}=1.5V.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 24
In a DC circuit at steady state, a capacitor $C$ is in series with a resistor $R$ across a battery $\mathcal E$. The current through this branch is:
**(A)** $0$ **(B)** $\mathcal E/R$ **(C)** $\mathcal E/2R$ **(D)** infinite

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

At steady state the capacitor is fully charged ⇒ acts as an **open circuit** ⇒ no current. **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 25
A capacitor $C=4\,\mu\text F$ charges through $R=5\,\text{k}\Omega$. The time constant is:
**(A)** $20\,\text{ms}$ **(B)** $1.25\,\text{ms}$ **(C)** $20\,\text s$ **(D)** $2\,\text{ms}$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$\tau=RC=(5000)(4\times10^{-6})=2\times10^{-2}\,\text s=20\,\text{ms}.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

## SECTION F — Mixed Concept

### Problem 26
The plates of a charged isolated capacitor are pulled apart (increasing $d$). Which quantity stays constant?
**(A)** Charge $Q$ **(B)** Voltage $V$ **(C)** Capacitance $C$ **(D)** Energy $U$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Isolated ⇒ $Q$ fixed. $C$ falls ($\propto1/d$), $V=Q/C$ rises, $U=Q^2/2C$ rises (you do work pulling apart). **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 27
In Problem 26, while $d$ is increased the electric field between the plates:
**(A)** stays constant **(B)** increases **(C)** decreases **(D)** becomes zero

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$E=\sigma/\varepsilon_0=Q/(\varepsilon_0A)$ depends only on $Q$ and $A$, both fixed ⇒ $E$ constant. **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 28
A spherical drop of capacitance $C$ is formed by merging $8$ identical small drops. The capacitance of the big drop is:
**(A)** $2C$ **(B)** $8C$ **(C)** $4C$ **(D)** $C/2$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Volume conserved: $R=8^{1/3}r=2r$. Since $C\propto R$, big drop $=2C.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 29
A metal slab of thickness $t$ is inserted between plates of gap $d$. The capacitance becomes:
**(A)** $\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{d-t}$ **(B)** $\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{d+t}$ **(C)** $\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{t}$ **(D)** $\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{d}$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

Metal slab = $K\to\infty$: it deletes its own thickness from the gap, $C=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0A}{d-t}$ (position irrelevant). **Answer: (A)**
</details>

### Problem 30
The dimensional formula of capacitance is:
**(A)** $\mathrm{M^{-1}L^{-2}T^{4}I^{2}}$ **(B)** $\mathrm{ML^2T^{-2}I}$ **(C)** $\mathrm{M^{-1}L^{-2}T^{2}I}$ **(D)** $\mathrm{MLT^{-2}}$

<details>
<summary>**Solution**</summary>

$[C]=[Q]/[V]=\dfrac{\mathrm{IT}}{\mathrm{ML^2T^{-3}I^{-1}}}=\mathrm{M^{-1}L^{-2}T^4I^2}.$ **Answer: (A)**
</details>
