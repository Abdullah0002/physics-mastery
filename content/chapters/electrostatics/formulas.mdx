## Fundamental Constants

| Symbol | Value | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| $e$ | $1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C | Elementary charge |
| $k$ | $9 \times 10^9$ N·m²·C⁻² | Coulomb constant |
| $\varepsilon_0$ | $8.85 \times 10^{-12}$ C²·N⁻¹·m⁻² | Permittivity of free space |

---

## Coulomb's Law

<Formula label="Coulomb's Force">
$$F = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} = \frac{q_1 q_2}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r^2}$$
</Formula>

---

## Electric Field

<Formula label="Field from a Point Charge">
$$E = k\frac{Q}{r^2}$$
</Formula>

| Charge Distribution | $E$ |
|---|---|
| Infinite line (charge density $\lambda$) | $\dfrac{\lambda}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 r}$ |
| Infinite plane (charge density $\sigma$) | $\dfrac{\sigma}{2\varepsilon_0}$ |
| Outside sphere (charge $Q$, radius $R$) | $\dfrac{kQ}{r^2}$ |
| Inside uniformly charged sphere | $\dfrac{kQr}{R^3}$ |
| Just outside conductor surface | $\dfrac{\sigma}{\varepsilon_0}$ |
| Inside conductor | $0$ |

---

## Gauss's Law

<Formula label="Gauss's Law">
$$\oint \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{Q_{enc}}{\varepsilon_0}$$
</Formula>

---

## Electric Potential

<Formula label="Potential from Point Charge">
$$V = k\frac{Q}{r}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="E–V Relation">
$$E = -\frac{dV}{dr}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Work done by external agent">
$$W = q(V_f - V_i) = q\,\Delta V$$
</Formula>

---

## Potential Energy

<Formula label="Two Point Charges">
$$U = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r}$$
</Formula>

---

## Electric Dipole

<Formula label="Dipole Moment">
$$p = q \cdot 2a$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Axial Field">
$$E_{axial} = \frac{2kp}{r^3}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Equatorial Field">
$$E_{eq} = \frac{kp}{r^3}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Torque in Uniform Field">
$$\tau = pE\sin\theta$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Potential Energy of Dipole">
$$U = -pE\cos\theta = -\vec{p}\cdot\vec{E}$$
</Formula>

---

## Capacitance

<Formula label="Capacitance Definition">
$$C = \frac{Q}{V}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Parallel Plate (vacuum)">
$$C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Parallel Plate (dielectric κ)">
$$C = \frac{\kappa\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Energy Stored">
$$U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{Q^2}{2C} = \frac{QV}{2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Energy Density in Field">
$$u = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$$
</Formula>

### Capacitor Combinations

**Series:**
$$\frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \cdots$$

**Parallel:**
$$C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2 + \cdots$$

## Quick Reference: Key Results

| Result | Formula |
|---|---|
| Charge ratio on connected spheres | $q_1 : q_2 = r_1 : r_2$ |
| Surface charge density ratio | $\sigma_1 : \sigma_2 = r_2 : r_1$ |
| No field inside hollow conductor | $E_{inside} = 0$ |
| Axial field = 2 × equatorial field | $E_{ax} = 2E_{eq}$ |
| Force on conductor surface | $\frac{\sigma^2}{2\varepsilon_0}$ per unit area |

<Callout type="tip">
For JEE, always check whether a question involves a conductor (charge on surface, $E$ inside = 0) or an insulator (charge throughout the volume). The approach differs completely.
</Callout>
