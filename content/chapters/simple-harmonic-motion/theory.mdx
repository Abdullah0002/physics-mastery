## 1. Definition of SHM

A particle executes SHM if its restoring force is proportional to displacement and directed toward equilibrium:

<Formula label="Restoring Force (SHM condition)">
$$F = -kx, \quad a = -\omega^2 x$$
</Formula>

where $\omega = \sqrt{k/m}$ = angular frequency.

---

## 2. Equations of Motion

Standard solution to $\ddot{x} = -\omega^2 x$:

$$x(t) = A\sin(\omega t + \phi)$$

where $A$ = amplitude, $\phi$ = initial phase.

<Formula label="Velocity in SHM">
$$v = \frac{dx}{dt} = A\omega\cos(\omega t + \phi) = \omega\sqrt{A^2 - x^2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Acceleration in SHM">
$$a = -\omega^2 x = -\omega^2 A\sin(\omega t + \phi)$$
</Formula>

---

## 3. Useful Relations

| Quantity | Formula |
|---|---|
| Angular frequency | $\omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi/T$ |
| Time period | $T = 2\pi/\omega$ |
| Max velocity | $v_{max} = A\omega$ (at $x=0$) |
| Max acceleration | $a_{max} = A\omega^2$ (at $x=\pm A$) |

---

## 4. Energy in SHM

<Formula label="Kinetic Energy">
$$K = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2(A^2 - x^2)$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Potential Energy">
$$U = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 x^2 = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Total Mechanical Energy">
$$E = K + U = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 A^2 = \frac{1}{2}kA^2 = \text{constant}$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="tip">
Total energy is constant and proportional to $A^2$. At equilibrium ($x=0$): $K = E$, $U = 0$. At extreme position ($x = \pm A$): $K = 0$, $U = E$.
</Callout>

## 5. Spring Systems

### Single Spring

$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$$

### Series Springs

$$\frac{1}{k_{eff}} = \frac{1}{k_1} + \frac{1}{k_2}, \quad T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k_{eff}}}$$

### Parallel Springs

$$k_{eff} = k_1 + k_2$$

### Spring Hanging Vertically

The equilibrium shifts downward by $mg/k$. The time period is the same as for horizontal spring.

## 6. Simple Pendulum

For small oscillations ($\theta < 15Â°$):

<Formula label="Time Period â€” Simple Pendulum">
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$
</Formula>

- Independent of mass and amplitude (for small $\theta$).
- $T$ increases if $g$ decreases (e.g., at higher altitude or in a lift accelerating downward).

**Effective $g$ in non-inertial frame:**
- Lift accelerating up: $g_{eff} = g + a$, $T$ decreases.
- Lift accelerating down: $g_{eff} = g - a$, $T$ increases.
- Free fall: $T \to \infty$ (weightlessness).

---

## 7. Physical Pendulum

Any rigid body oscillating about a pivot:

$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{I}{Mg\,d}}$$

where $I$ = moment of inertia about pivot, $d$ = distance from pivot to CoM.

---

## 8. Compound / Equivalent Pendulum

The simple pendulum of equivalent length $L_{eq}$:

$$L_{eq} = \frac{I}{Md} = d + \frac{I_{cm}}{Md}$$

---

## 9. Superposition of Two SHMs

Two SHMs along the same line, same frequency:

$$x_1 = A_1\sin(\omega t), \quad x_2 = A_2\sin(\omega t + \delta)$$

Resultant:

$$A_R = \sqrt{A_1^2 + A_2^2 + 2A_1A_2\cos\delta}, \quad \tan\phi = \frac{A_2\sin\delta}{A_1 + A_2\cos\delta}$$

- $\delta = 0$: $A_R = A_1 + A_2$ (constructive)
- $\delta = \pi$: $A_R = |A_1 - A_2|$ (destructive)

---

## 10. Damped Oscillations

With damping force $-b\dot{x}$, the amplitude decays:

$$x(t) = A_0\,e^{-bt/2m}\cos(\omega_d t + \phi)$$

$$\omega_d = \sqrt{\omega_0^2 - \left(\frac{b}{2m}\right)^2} < \omega_0$$

**Quality factor:** $Q = \omega_0 m/b$.

---

## 11. Resonance

When driving frequency $= \omega_0$ (natural frequency), amplitude is maximum. With damping, maximum amplitude occurs just below $\omega_0$.

---

## 12. SHM in Different Physical Systems

| System | Period |
|---|---|
| Mass on spring ($k$) | $2\pi\sqrt{m/k}$ |
| Simple pendulum | $2\pi\sqrt{L/g}$ |
| Physical pendulum | $2\pi\sqrt{I/(Mgd)}$ |
| LC circuit | $2\pi\sqrt{LC}$ |
| Torsional pendulum | $2\pi\sqrt{I/C}$ where $C$ = torsional constant |
