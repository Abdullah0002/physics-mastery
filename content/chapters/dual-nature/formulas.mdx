## Photoelectric Effect

<Formula label="Einstein's Photoelectric Equation">
$$h\nu = \phi_0 + KE_{\max} = \phi_0 + eV_s$$
$$KE_{\max} = h\nu - h\nu_0 = h(\nu - \nu_0)$$
$$\text{(} \phi_0 = h\nu_0 \text{ = work function, } \nu_0 \text{ = threshold frequency)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Stopping Potential">
$$V_s = \frac{KE_{\max}}{e} = \frac{h(\nu - \nu_0)}{e}$$
$$eV_s = h\nu - \phi_0$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Threshold Wavelength and Frequency">
$$\phi_0 = h\nu_0 = \frac{hc}{\lambda_0}$$
$$\lambda_0 = \frac{hc}{\phi_0} = \frac{1240\ \text{eV·nm}}{\phi_0(\text{eV})}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Maximum Kinetic Energy">
$$KE_{\max} = \frac{1}{2}mv_{\max}^2 = eV_s$$
$$v_{\max} = \sqrt{\frac{2eV_s}{m}}$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
$KE_{\max}$ depends only on frequency (not intensity) of light. Photoelectric current depends on intensity.
</Callout>

## Photon Properties

<Formula label="Photon Energy">
$$E = h\nu = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
$$h = 6.626\times10^{-34}\ \text{J·s} = 4.136\times10^{-15}\ \text{eV·s}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Photon Momentum">
$$p = \frac{h}{\lambda} = \frac{h\nu}{c} = \frac{E}{c}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Rest Mass of Photon">
$$m_0 = 0 \quad \text{(photon has no rest mass)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Useful Constant">
$$hc = 1240\ \text{eV·nm} = 12400\ \text{eV·Å}$$
</Formula>

## de Broglie Hypothesis

<Formula label="de Broglie Wavelength">
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="de Broglie Wavelength from Kinetic Energy">
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mKE}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="de Broglie Wavelength of Accelerated Electron">
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2meV}} = \frac{1.227}{\sqrt{V}}\ \text{nm} \quad \text{(V in volts)}$$
$$\lambda(\text{Å}) = \frac{12.27}{\sqrt{V}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Thermal de Broglie Wavelength">
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{3mk_BT}} = \frac{h}{\sqrt{3mRT/N_A}}$$
</Formula>

## Davisson-Germer Experiment

<Formula label="Bragg Condition (Electron Diffraction)">
$$2d\sin\theta = n\lambda$$
$$\text{(d = lattice spacing, confirmed wave nature of electrons)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Davisson-Germer — Peak at 54 V">
$$\lambda_{\text{dB}} = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2meV}} \approx 0.167\ \text{nm at } 54\ \text{V}$$
$$\text{matches Bragg scattering from Ni crystal}$$
</Formula>

## Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

<Formula label="Uncertainty Principle (Position-Momentum)">
$$\Delta x \cdot \Delta p_x \geq \frac{h}{4\pi} = \frac{\hbar}{2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Uncertainty Principle (Energy-Time)">
$$\Delta E \cdot \Delta t \geq \frac{h}{4\pi} = \frac{\hbar}{2}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Uncertainty Principle (Angular)">
$$\Delta L \cdot \Delta\theta \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}$$
</Formula>

## Compton Effect

<Formula label="Compton Shift">
$$\Delta\lambda = \lambda' - \lambda = \frac{h}{m_e c}(1-\cos\phi) = \lambda_c(1-\cos\phi)$$
$$\lambda_c = \frac{h}{m_e c} = 2.426\ \text{pm} \quad \text{(Compton wavelength)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Maximum Compton Shift">
$$\Delta\lambda_{\max} = 2\lambda_c = \frac{2h}{m_e c} = 4.85\ \text{pm} \quad (\phi = 180°)$$
</Formula>
