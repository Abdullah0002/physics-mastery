## Nuclear Size and Composition

<Formula label="Nuclear Radius">
$$R = R_0 A^{1/3}, \quad R_0 = 1.2\ \text{fm} = 1.2\times10^{-15}\ \text{m}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Nuclear Volume">
$$V = \frac{4}{3}\pi R^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi R_0^3 A \propto A$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Nuclear Density">
$$\rho = \frac{3m_u}{4\pi R_0^3} \approx 2.3\times10^{17}\ \text{kg m}^{-3} \quad \text{(same for all nuclei)}$$
</Formula>

## Mass Defect and Binding Energy

<Formula label="Mass Defect">
$$\Delta m = \left[Zm_p + (A-Z)m_n\right] - M_{\text{nucleus}}$$
$$\text{(in atomic masses: } \Delta m = Zm_H + (A-Z)m_n - M_{\text{atom}}\text{)}$$
$$1\ u = 931.5\ \text{MeV}/c^2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Binding Energy">
$$BE = \Delta m \cdot c^2 = \Delta m \times 931.5\ \text{MeV} \quad (\Delta m \text{ in u})$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Binding Energy per Nucleon">
$$BE/A = \frac{BE}{A}$$
$$\text{Maximum around } A = 56 \text{ (Fe-56): BE/A} \approx 8.8\ \text{MeV}$$
$$\text{Fe-56 is most stable nucleus}$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
BE/A increases from light to medium nuclei (fusion releases energy) and decreases from medium to heavy nuclei (fission releases energy).
</Callout>

## Nuclear Forces

<Formula label="Range of Nuclear Force">
$$r \lesssim 10^{-15}\ \text{m} = 1\ \text{fm}$$
$$\text{Nuclear force is short-range, charge-independent, spin-dependent}$$
</Formula>

## Radioactive Decay Law

<Formula label="Radioactive Decay Law">
$$N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$$
$$A(t) = A_0 e^{-\lambda t} \quad \text{(A = activity)}$$
$$A = \lambda N$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Half-Life">
$$T_{1/2} = \frac{\ln 2}{\lambda} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda}$$
$$N = N_0\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{t/T_{1/2}} = \frac{N_0}{2^n} \quad \text{(n = number of half-lives)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Mean / Average Life">
$$\tau = \frac{1}{\lambda} = \frac{T_{1/2}}{\ln 2} = \frac{T_{1/2}}{0.693} = 1.44\,T_{1/2}$$
$$N = N_0 e^{-t/\tau}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Activity">
$$A = -\frac{dN}{dt} = \lambda N = \frac{N_0\ln 2}{T_{1/2}}e^{-\lambda t}$$
$$1\ \text{Ci} = 3.7\times10^{10}\ \text{disintegrations s}^{-1}$$
$$1\ \text{Bq} = 1\ \text{disintegration s}^{-1}$$
</Formula>

## Types of Radioactive Decay

<Formula label="Alpha Decay">
$${}^A_Z X \to {}^{A-4}_{Z-2} Y + {}^4_2\text{He} + Q$$
$$Q = [m_X - m_Y - m_\alpha]c^2$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Beta-minus Decay">
$${}^A_Z X \to {}^A_{Z+1} Y + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e + Q$$
$$n \to p + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Beta-plus Decay">
$${}^A_Z X \to {}^A_{Z-1} Y + e^+ + \nu_e + Q$$
$$p \to n + e^+ + \nu_e$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Gamma Decay">
$${}^A_Z X^* \to {}^A_Z X + \gamma$$
$$\text{(no change in Z or A; nucleus de-excites)}$$
</Formula>

## Q-Value of Nuclear Reaction

<Formula label="Q-Value">
$$Q = (m_{\text{reactants}} - m_{\text{products}})c^2$$
$$Q > 0 \text{: exothermic (energy released)}$$
$$Q < 0 \text{: endothermic (energy required)}$$
</Formula>

## Nuclear Fission and Fusion

<Formula label="Typical Fission (U-235)">
$${}^{235}_{92}\text{U} + {}^1_0n \to {}^{141}_{56}\text{Ba} + {}^{92}_{36}\text{Kr} + 3{}^1_0n + 200\ \text{MeV}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Typical Fusion (D-T)">
$${}^2_1\text{H} + {}^3_1\text{H} \to {}^4_2\text{He} + {}^1_0n + 17.6\ \text{MeV}$$
</Formula>

## Radioactive Series

<Formula label="Secular Equilibrium">
$$\lambda_1 N_1 = \lambda_2 N_2 \implies A_1 = A_2 \quad \text{(when } T_1 \gg T_2\text{)}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Simultaneous Decay Modes">
$$\lambda_{\text{eff}} = \lambda_1 + \lambda_2, \quad T_{\text{eff}} = \frac{T_1 T_2}{T_1 + T_2}$$
</Formula>
