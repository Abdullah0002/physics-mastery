## Dimensional Analysis — Checking & Deriving

<Formula label="Principle of Homogeneity">
$$\text{Dimensions of LHS} = \text{Dimensions of RHS}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Period of Simple Pendulum (derived)">
$$T \propto l^a\, g^b \implies [T] = [L]^a [LT^{-2}]^b \implies a = \tfrac{1}{2},\ b = -\tfrac{1}{2}$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="tip">
Dimensional analysis cannot determine dimensionless constants (like $2\pi$), and it fails when a quantity depends on more than 3 unknowns involving M, L, T.
</Callout>

## Error Analysis

<Formula label="Absolute Error">
$$\Delta a_i = |a_{\text{mean}} - a_i|, \quad \overline{\Delta a} = \frac{\sum |\Delta a_i|}{n}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Relative / Fractional Error">
$$\frac{\overline{\Delta a}}{a_{\text{mean}}}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Percentage Error">
$$\text{Percentage error} = \frac{\overline{\Delta a}}{a_{\text{mean}}} \times 100\%$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Error in Sum / Difference">
$$Z = A \pm B \implies \Delta Z = \Delta A + \Delta B$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Error in Product / Quotient">
$$Z = \frac{A^p B^q}{C^r} \implies \frac{\Delta Z}{Z} = p\frac{\Delta A}{A} + q\frac{\Delta B}{B} + r\frac{\Delta C}{C}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Error in Power">
$$Z = A^n \implies \frac{\Delta Z}{Z} = n\frac{\Delta A}{A}$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
In subtraction of nearly equal quantities, relative error can be very large — a major source of experimental error.
</Callout>

## Significant Figures

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| All non-zero digits significant | 2345 → 4 sig. figs |
| Zeros between non-zero digits | 2005 → 4 sig. figs |
| Leading zeros NOT significant | 0.0045 → 2 sig. figs |
| Trailing zeros after decimal | 3.200 → 4 sig. figs |
| Trailing zeros before decimal (ambiguous) | 2500 → 2 or 4 (use sci. notation) |

<Formula label="Rounding Rule">
$$\text{If dropped digit} > 5 \Rightarrow \text{round up};\quad = 5 \Rightarrow \text{round to even}$$
</Formula>
