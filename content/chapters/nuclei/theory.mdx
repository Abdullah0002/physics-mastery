## 1. Nuclear Structure

A nucleus has $Z$ protons and $N$ neutrons; mass number $A = Z + N$.

**Nuclear radius:**

<Formula label="Nuclear Radius">
$$R = R_0 A^{1/3}, \quad R_0 \approx 1.2\ \text{fm} = 1.2\times10^{-15}\ \text{m}$$
</Formula>

**Nuclear density:** Nearly constant for all nuclei ($\approx 2.3\times10^{17}\ \text{kg/m}^3$). This is because $R \propto A^{1/3}$ and volume $\propto A$.

---

## 2. Mass Defect and Binding Energy

**Mass defect:**

$$\Delta m = Zm_p + Nm_n - M_{nucleus}$$

where $M_{nucleus}$ = mass of the nucleus.

<Formula label="Binding Energy">
$$BE = \Delta m\cdot c^2 = \Delta m\times931.5\ \text{MeV/u}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Binding Energy per Nucleon">
$$BE/A = \frac{\Delta m\cdot c^2}{A}$$
</Formula>

<Callout type="important">
$BE/A$ peaks around iron (Fe, $A=56$) at ~8.8 MeV. Lighter nuclei gain energy by **fusion**; heavier by **fission** — both release energy.
</Callout>

---

## 3. Nuclear Forces

- Strong nuclear force: extremely strong, short-range ($\sim 1-3$ fm), charge-independent.
- Overcomes electrostatic repulsion between protons.
- Attractive for $r > 0.7$ fm; repulsive for $r < 0.7$ fm.

---

## 4. Radioactive Decay

Three types:

| Decay | Emission | Change in $Z$, $A$ |
|---|---|---|
| Alpha ($\alpha$) | ${}^4_2\text{He}$ nucleus | $Z-2$, $A-4$ |
| Beta minus ($\beta^-$) | Electron + antineutrino | $Z+1$, $A$ unchanged |
| Beta plus ($\beta^+$) | Positron + neutrino | $Z-1$, $A$ unchanged |
| Gamma ($\gamma$) | High-energy photon | $Z$, $A$ unchanged |

---

## 5. Radioactive Decay Law

<Formula label="Activity">
$$N(t) = N_0\,e^{-\lambda t}, \quad A(t) = \lambda N(t) = A_0\,e^{-\lambda t}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Half-life">
$$T_{1/2} = \frac{\ln 2}{\lambda} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda}$$
</Formula>

<Formula label="Mean life">
$$\tau = \frac{1}{\lambda} = \frac{T_{1/2}}{0.693} = 1.44\,T_{1/2}$$
</Formula>

**Activity:** $A = \lambda N$ (SI unit: becquerel, Bq = 1 decay/s; also curie: $1\ \text{Ci} = 3.7\times10^{10}\ \text{Bq}$).

---

## 6. Successive Decay (Decay Series)

If parent $A \to$ daughter $B \to$ stable $C$:

**Secular equilibrium:** When $T_{1/2,A} \gg T_{1/2,B}$, activity of $A \approx$ activity of $B$ after long time.

---

## 7. Nuclear Fission

Heavy nucleus splits into two lighter nuclei + neutrons + energy.

$${}^{235}_{92}\text{U} + {}^1_0\text{n} \to {}^{141}_{56}\text{Ba} + {}^{92}_{36}\text{Kr} + 3{}^1_0\text{n} + \text{energy}$$

**Energy released:** ~200 MeV per fission of ${}^{235}$U.

**Chain reaction:** If $k\geq1$ (each fission triggers $\geq1$ more), self-sustaining reaction occurs. Critical mass controls this.

---

## 8. Nuclear Fusion

Light nuclei combine to form heavier nucleus + energy.

$${}^2_1\text{H} + {}^3_1\text{H} \to {}^4_2\text{He} + {}^1_0\text{n} + 17.6\ \text{MeV}$$

Requires extremely high temperature (~$10^7$ K) to overcome Coulomb barrier — **thermonuclear reaction**.

**Energy per nucleon** from fusion $>$ fission.

---

## 9. Q-value of a Nuclear Reaction

$$Q = (m_{reactants} - m_{products})c^2$$

If $Q > 0$: exothermic (energy released). If $Q < 0$: endothermic (energy required).
