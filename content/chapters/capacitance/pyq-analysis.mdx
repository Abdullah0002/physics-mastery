## 3. The Highest-Yield Concepts (study these first)

<Callout type="important">
If you have limited time, **master these five** — they cover the large majority of Capacitance marks:
1. **The Q-fixed vs V-fixed decision** and its table (Section 14 of Theory).
2. **Series/parallel** identification and voltage/charge division.
3. **Energy: $\tfrac12CV^2 = Q^2/2C = \tfrac12QV$** and which form to use when.
4. **Charge sharing** common potential + guaranteed energy loss.
5. **Capacitor = open circuit at DC steady state** (for finding final charge in mixed R–C networks).
</Callout>

## 4. Repeated Question Patterns

<Callout type="derivation">
**These templates recur almost every cycle — recognise them instantly:**

- **"Battery connected vs disconnected, then insert dielectric — find new $Q/V/E/U$."** The single most repeated pattern. Always start by stating what's fixed.
- **"Two charged capacitors connected — find common potential and heat lost."** Direct formula plug-in.
- **"Network reduces by series/parallel — find $C_{eq}$ or charge on one cap."** Redraw, label nodes.
- **"Slab of thickness $t<d$ inserted — find new $C$."** Use $C=\varepsilon_0A/[d-t(1-1/K)]$.
- **"Steady-state circuit with a capacitor branch — find charge on it."** Open the cap, solve resistive circuit, then $Q=CV$.
- **"Plates pulled apart while isolated — what changes?"** $Q,E$ constant; $V,U$ rise.
- **(Advanced) Cube/bridge/infinite-ladder $C_{eq}$.** Symmetry or self-similarity.
- **(Advanced) Force on dielectric via energy method.**
</Callout>

## 5. Trend Notes by Exam

### JEE Main
- Strongly **formula-driven**; one numerical + occasionally one statement/assertion-reason.
- Favourite: dielectric insertion factor changes, energy comparison, simple combinations.
- Time budget: these should take **under 90 s each** — they are designed as "secure marks."

### JEE Advanced
- Capacitance is usually **embedded** in a larger electrostatics/circuit problem.
- Expect **multiple-correct** (conceptual energy/field statements) and **integer** answers (network $C_{eq}$, % energy lost).
- Multi-dielectric slabs and **force-by-energy-method** distinguish top scorers.

### NEET
- Almost entirely **single-concept, single-formula**: definition of $C$, $C=K\varepsilon_0A/d$, energy $\tfrac12CV^2$, simple series/parallel.
- Conceptual one-liners: effect of dielectric, effect of separation.

## 6. Common Trap Themes the Examiners Love

<Callout type="warning">
- **The energy paradox:** dielectric raises energy (battery on) but lowers it (battery off). Tested relentlessly.
- **Battery work ≠ stored energy:** the missing 50%.
- **Voltage division in series** (smaller cap, bigger $V$) — counterintuitive, hence examined.
- **Charge sharing energy loss** even with ideal wires.
- **Balanced-bridge distractor:** a big bridge capacitor that actually carries zero charge.
- **Steady-state current "through" a capacitor** = zero.
</Callout>

## 7. Smart Preparation Strategy

<Callout type="tip">
1. **Lock the Q-fixed/V-fixed table** until it is automatic — it alone secures the most-repeated question type.
2. **Drill 20–30 network reductions** so series/parallel identification is instant.
3. **Memorise the energy trio** and the charge-sharing loss formula.
4. For Advanced, **practice 10 symmetry/energy-method problems** (cube, bridge, ladder, dielectric force).
5. **Always run a limiting-case check** ($K\to1$, $t\to0$, $b\to\infty$) on derived expressions.
6. Solve **last 10 years' PYQs** of this chapter timed; you will see the same 6–7 templates repeat.
</Callout>

</Callout>
